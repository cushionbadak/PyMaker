<code class="descclassname">ssl.</code><code class="descname">RAND_egd</code><span class="sig-paren">(</span><em>path</em><span class="sig-paren">)</span><a class="headerlink" href="#ssl.RAND_egd" title="Permalink to this definition">Â¶</a></dt>
<dd><p>If you are running an entropy-gathering daemon (EGD) somewhere, and <em>path</em>
is the pathname of a socket connection open to it, this will read 256 bytes
of randomness from the socket, and add it to the SSL pseudo-random number
generator to increase the security of generated secret keys.  This is
typically only necessary on systems without better sources of randomness.</p>
<p>See <a class="reference external" href="http://egd.sourceforge.net/">http://egd.sourceforge.net/</a> or <a class="reference external" href="http://prngd.sourceforge.net/">http://prngd.sourceforge.net/</a> for sources
of entropy-gathering daemons.</p>
<p class="availability"><a class="reference internal" href="intro.html#availability"><span class="std std-ref">Availability</span></a>: not available with LibreSSL and OpenSSL &gt; 1.1.0.</p>
</dd>
