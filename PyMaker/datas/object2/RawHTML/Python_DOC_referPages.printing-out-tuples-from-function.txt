<p>the reason you are printing them one by one is that you are printing them one by one inside the loop, to get all of them at once you will need to create a list and print that at the end of the loop. there are several ways to go about this and m0nte-cr1st0 gave you the most legible one, ill give you something a little shorter:<br/></p>
<pre><code>def my_enumerate(items):
    return list(zip(range(len(items)), items))
</code></pre>
<p>zip lets you smoosh together 2 lists pairwise<br/>
this solution of course assumes you cant just use the built in <code>enumerate</code></p>
