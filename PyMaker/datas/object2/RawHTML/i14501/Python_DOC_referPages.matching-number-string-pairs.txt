<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>I adapted the <a href="http://www.regular-expressions.info/floatingpoint.html" rel="nofollow">Floating Point Numbers with a Regular Expression</a> regex for you and shortened the regex a bit (note the alternative list is gone that means less backtracking, and the <code>(?i)</code> case insensitive matching option in front to turn <code>[A-Za-z]</code> into <code>[a-z]</code>):</p>
<pre><code>import re
s = "R10666: 273141 C1 + 273141 C2 + 273141 C3 + 273141 C4 + 273141 C5 - 273141 C6"
print re.findall(r'(?i)([-+]?\s*\d*\.?\d+(?:[eE][-+]?\d+)?)(\s+\w+)', s)
</code></pre>
<p>Output of the <a href="http://ideone.com/QzK9NM" rel="nofollow">IDEONE demo</a>:</p>
<pre><code>[(' 273141', ' C1'), ('+ 273141', ' C2'), ('+ 273141', ' C3'), ('+ 273141', ' C4'), ('+ 273141', ' C5'), ('- 273141', ' C6')]
</code></pre>
</div>
<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p><a href="https://docs.python.org/3/library/re.html#re.findall" rel="nofollow"><code>findall</code></a> will put all the submatches in the results. In your case, the empty strings come from the unmatched decimals if they are present; so use non-capture groups instead:</p>
<pre><code>([+-]? \d+(?:\.\d*)?|\.\d+(?:[eE][+-]?\d+)?) ([a-zA-Z0-9_]+)
</code></pre>
<p>I also moved the space at the second capture group outside so you don't get that leading space.</p>
