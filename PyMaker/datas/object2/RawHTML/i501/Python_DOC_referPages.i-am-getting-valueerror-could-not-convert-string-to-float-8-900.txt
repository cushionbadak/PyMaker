<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>Depending on the <a href="https://docs.oracle.com/cd/E19455-01/806-0169/overview-9/index.html" rel="nofollow noreferrer">numeric convention</a> used, a comma can either denote a thousands separator or a decimal point.</p>
<p><a href="https://docs.python.org/3/reference/lexical_analysis.html#grammar-token-pointfloat" rel="nofollow noreferrer">Python <code>float</code> numbers syntax</a> uses a dot (<code>.</code>) as decimal point and an optional underscore (<code>_</code>) as thousand separator, for readability only.</p>
<p>This means you need to know which numerical convention the number you are reading uses.</p>
<p>In your case it would seem you are reading numbers using the <em>US-English</em> numeric convention.</p>
<blockquote>
<p>US-English | 4,294,967,295.00</p>
</blockquote>
<p>All you need is to remove the comma which acts as thousands separator.</p>
<pre><code>price_without_symbol=price[1:].replace(',', '')
print(float(price_without_symbol))
</code></pre>
</div>
