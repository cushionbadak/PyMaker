<p>It wouldn't be good to do that, but the best way is to use a dictionary:</p>
<pre><code>d={}
for k in final_test:
    d['url_'+k] = test_url + k
</code></pre>
<p>Or much better in a dictionary comprehension:</p>
<pre><code>d={'url_'+k:test_url + k for k in final_test}
</code></pre>
<p>And now:</p>
<pre><code>print(d)
</code></pre>
<p>Both reproduce:</p>
<pre><code>{'url_v001': 'https://0.0.0.0/api/1.1/volumev001', 'url_v002': 'https://0.0.0.0/api/1.1/volumev002', 'url_v003': 'https://0.0.0.0/api/1.1/volumev003', 'url_v004': 'https://0.0.0.0/api/1.1/volumev004'}
</code></pre>
<p>To use <code>d</code>:</p>
<pre><code>for k,v in d.items():
    print(k+',',v)
</code></pre>
<p>Outputs:</p>
<pre><code>url_v001, https://0.0.0.0/api/1.1/volumev001
