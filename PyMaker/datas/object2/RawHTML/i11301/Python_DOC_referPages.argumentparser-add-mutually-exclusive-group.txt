<p>I came across this method in Python:</p>
<pre><code>ArgumentParser.add_mutually_exclusive_group
</code></pre>
<p>In the description, it mentioned:</p>
<pre><code>Create a mutually exclusive group.
</code></pre>
<p>What does a <code>mutually exclusive group</code> mean in this context? Is there a simple example that clarifies this?</p>
<p>Thanks.</p>
</div>
<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>The <a href="https://docs.python.org/2/library/argparse.html" rel="nofollow">documentation</a> has an explanation (emphasis mine):</p>
<blockquote>
<p>Create a mutually exclusive group. <code>argparse</code> will make sure that <strong>only one</strong> of the arguments in the mutually exclusive group was present on the command line.</p>
</blockquote>
</div>
<span class="comment-copy">It means you can add arguments to the mutually exclusive group. For instance if <code>-a</code> and <code>-b</code> are in the group, you can supply either <code>-a</code> or <code>-b</code>, but not both. Take a look at Mutual exclusion here: <a href="https://docs.python.org/3/library/argparse.html" rel="nofollow noreferrer">docs.python.org/3/library/argparse.html</a></span>
