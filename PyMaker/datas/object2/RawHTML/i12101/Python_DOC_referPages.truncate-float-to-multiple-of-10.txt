<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>I have the number 67.14, for example.</p>
<p>I need to set another variable as the next multiple of 10 down (60, in this case).</p>
<p>Would it be possible to just get the "7.14" from "67.14" and take it away?</p>
</div>
<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>Use <a href="https://docs.python.org/3/library/stdtypes.html#numeric-types-int-float-complex" rel="nofollow"><code>//</code></a> to get the floored quotient of x and y:</p>
<pre><code>67.14 // 10 * 10
</code></pre>
<p>Result:</p>
<pre><code>60.0
</code></pre>
<p>Use <code>%</code> to get the remainder of x / y:</p>
<pre><code>67.14 % 10
</code></pre>
<p>Result:</p>
<pre><code>7.140000000000001
</code></pre>
</div>
<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>Type:</p>
<pre><code>n = 67.14
print n - n % 10

&gt;&gt; result is 60.0
</code></pre>
</div>
<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>The <code>%</code> (modulo) sign should help you here:</p>
<p><code>new = old - (old % 10)</code></p>
</div>
<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>There is an easier solution:</p>
<ul>
<li>divide by 10</li>
<li>round to integer</li>
<li><p>multiply by 10</p>
<pre><code>&gt;&gt; int(11.7/10)*10
10
</code></pre></li>
</ul>
</div>
