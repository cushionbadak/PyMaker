<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>Instead of using <code>find()</code>, you can just parse your <code>number_pick</code> to <code>int</code></p>
<p>And I will use <code>str.split()</code> method, which simply takes a <strong>string</strong> and <em>splits</em> it by a given separator.</p>
<pre><code>user_input = input("Your chosen number: ").split(' ')
# I use .split(' ') to make '5 4 4 6 1' into ['5', '4', '4', '6', '1']
# Because now it will be easier to index it

number_pick = int(input("Num: "))
# If we enter 4, this will be the integer 4, not '4'

# And now we just take the element with our index - 1,
# because lists are zero-indexed
value = user_input[number_pick - 1]

print("Value: {}".format(value))  # Value: 6
</code></pre>
<p>And after running this code:</p>
<pre><code>&gt;&gt;&gt; Your chosen number: 5 5 4 6 1
['5', '4', '4', '6', '1']
&gt;&gt;&gt; Num: 4
4

Value: 6
</code></pre>
<p>And I think <code>math</code> is redundant for this piece of code.</p>
<h3><code>split()</code> usage explanation:</h3>
<p>When we enter <code>5 4 4 6 1</code> for our <code>user_input</code> variable, it's value is simply the string <code>"5 4 4 6 1"</code>.</p>
<p>When we do <code>.split(' ')</code> on it, it creates a list with our values, separated by <code>" "</code>.</p>
<p>So after doing split, we have <code>['5', '4', '4', '6', '1']</code>. See <a href="https://docs.python.org/3/library/stdtypes.html#str.split" rel="nofollow noreferrer">the docs</a> for more information.</p>

<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>You have misunderstood the nature of the <code>find</code> option in <code>string</code> and the idea that it returns an index, with a <code>list</code>.<br/>
<code>find</code> determines if string str occurs in string and returns its <code>index</code> which is the starting point of <code>str</code> within the <code>string</code><br/>
So:</p>
<pre><code>&gt;&gt;&gt; a="now is the winter"
&gt;&gt;&gt; a.find("winter")
11
</code></pre>
<p>The word "winter" exists in a and starts at position 11 (starting from position zero)<br/>
In your case:</p>
<pre><code>&gt;&gt;&gt; a="5 5 4 6 1"
&gt;&gt;&gt; a.find("4")
4
</code></pre>
<p>is the correct answer.    </p>
<p>Extending this out, you can manipulate the results of the index from <code>find</code></p>
<pre><code>&gt;&gt;&gt; a="now is the winter of our discontent, made glorious Summer by this Son of York"
&gt;&gt;&gt; f="winter"
&gt;&gt;&gt; x = a.find(f)
&gt;&gt;&gt; a[x:]
'winter of our discontent, made glorious Summer by this Son of York'
&gt;&gt;&gt; a[x:len(f)+x]
'winter'
</code></pre>
<p>Best run with the answer that converts it to a list, as that appears to be what you actually wanted to do.</p>
