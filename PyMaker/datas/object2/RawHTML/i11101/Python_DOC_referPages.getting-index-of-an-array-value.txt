<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>Take a look at numpy.where in the docs</p>
<pre><code>np.where(e&amp;f)[0]
</code></pre>
<p>Outputs:</p>
<pre><code> array([0, 3])
</code></pre>
</div>
<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>You can just use a list comprehension to pick them out:</p>
<pre><code>&gt;&gt;&gt; e = np.array([True, False, True, True])    
&gt;&gt;&gt; f = np.array([True, False, False, True])
&gt;&gt;&gt; [i for i,v in enumerate(e&amp;f, 1) if v]
[1, 4]
</code></pre>
<p>Using <a href="https://docs.python.org/3/library/functions.html#enumerate" rel="nofollow"><code>enumerate()</code></a> you can specify the initial index, in this case 1.</p>
</div>
<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>or simply this </p>
<pre><code>np.where(e&amp;f)
</code></pre>
</div>
