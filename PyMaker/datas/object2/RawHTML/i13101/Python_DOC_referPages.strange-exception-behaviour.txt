<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p><code>IOError</code> is only an alias for <code>OSError</code> in Python3, see <a href="https://docs.python.org/3/library/exceptions.html#concrete-exceptions" rel="noreferrer">https://docs.python.org/3/library/exceptions.html#concrete-exceptions</a>:</p>
<blockquote>
<p>Changed in version 3.3: <code>EnvironmentError</code>, <code>IOError</code>, <code>WindowsError</code>,
  <code>VMSError</code>, <code>socket.error</code>, <code>select.error</code> and <code>mmap.error</code> have been
  merged into <code>OSError</code>, and the constructor may return a subclass.</p>
</blockquote>
