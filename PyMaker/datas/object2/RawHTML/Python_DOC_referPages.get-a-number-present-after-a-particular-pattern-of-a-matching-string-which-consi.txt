<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>A small change is needed for you to get your desired output. In your,</p>
<pre><code>my_list = ['Ref.:', 'Reference', 'tramite', 'Expediente', 'Expediente No', 'Expedien N°', 'Exp.No', 'Expedien', 'Expedien N0']
</code></pre>
<p>You have specified <code>Expedien</code> before <code>Expedien N0</code> due to which in <code>text Expedien N0 18-00232995</code> text <code>Expedien</code> matches and <code>N0</code> matches in second group and leaves no scope for matching <code>Expedien N0</code> as it comes later. Hence if you just change the order in your list and place <code>Expedien N0</code> before <code>Expedien</code>, then <code>Expedien N0</code> matches the first group and <code>18-00232995</code> gets captured in second group and gives you your desired results. Check your modified python code below,</p>
<pre><code>import re
s="""text Expedien N0 18-00232995
$cat input_file
some text before Expedien: 1-21-212-16-26 some random text
Reference RE9833 of all sentences.
abc
123
456
something blah blah Ref.: 
tramite  1234567
Ref.:
some junk Expedien N° 18-00777 # some new content
some text Expedien N°18-0022995 # some garbled content"""
my_list = ['Ref.:', 'Reference', 'tramite', 'Expediente', 'Expediente No', 'Expedien N°', 'Exp.No', 'Expedien N0', 'Expedien']

rx = r'(?&lt;!\w)({})\W*([A-Z]*\d+(?:-+[A-Z]*\d+)*)'.format('|'.join(map(re.escape,my_list)))
print(rx)
print(re.findall(rx, s))
</code></pre>
<p>Prints,</p>
<pre><code>[('Expedien N0', '18-00232995'), ('Expedien', '1-21-212-16-26'), ('Reference', 'RE9833'), ('tramite', '1234567'), ('Expedien N°', '18-00777'), ('Expedien N°', '18-0022995')]
 ^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^ You have your intended tuple here in your findall results
</code></pre>
