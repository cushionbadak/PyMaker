<pre><code>import re

x = ('"weather":[{"id":801,"main":"clouds","description":"clouds","icon":"')

y = re.search(r'"main":"\w+"', str(x))
c = y.group(0)
z = re.search(r'\w+', str(c))

<p>you can just specify a group in your original pattern:</p>
<pre><code>import re

x = ('"weather":[{"id":801,"main":"clouds","description":"clouds","icon":"')

y = re.search(r'"main":"(\w+)"', str(x))
c = y.group(1)

print(y)
print(c)
</code></pre>
<p>but the comments are stating a good point that you could probably just <code>json</code> library to parse your text if it is a json.</p>

<p>To detect what follows <code>"main"</code>, you can try this:</p>
<pre><code>import re

x = ('"weather":[{"id":801,"main":"clouds","description":"clouds","icon":"')
final_x = re.findall('(?&lt;="main":)\s*"\w+"', x)
</code></pre>
<p>Output:</p>
<pre><code>['"clouds"']
</code></pre>
