<p>as ajcr wrote at the comment, <code>from_dict</code> is a method that belongs to dataframe and thus you can write the following to achieve your goal:</p>
<pre><code>from collections import Counter
import pandas as pd

repeaters = Counter({"nan": 1618, 'store@kiddicare.com': 265, 'testorders@worldstores.co.uk': 1})

dfr = pd.DataFrame.from_dict(repeaters, orient='index')
print dfr
</code></pre>
<p><strong>Output:</strong></p>
<pre><code>testorders@worldstores.co.uk     1
nan                           1618
store@kiddicare.com            265
</code></pre>
</div>
<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>Alternatively you could use <code>pd.Series.value_counts</code>, which returns a <code>Series</code> object.</p>
<pre><code>df.email_address.value_counts(dropna=False)
</code></pre>
<p>Sample output:</p>
<pre><code>b@y.com    2
a@x.com    1
NaN        1
dtype: int64
</code></pre>
<p>This is not exactly what you asked for but looks like what you'd like to achieve.</p>
