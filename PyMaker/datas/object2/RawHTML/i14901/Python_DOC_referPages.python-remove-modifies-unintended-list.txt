<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>it's because you copied the <strong>reference</strong> of <code>x</code> into <code>y</code>.
you can create a copy by <code>y = list(x)</code>. </p>
<p><strong>for example:</strong></p>
<pre><code>x=['0100', '1111', '1001', '1011']
y = list(x)
y.remove('0100')
print(x)
</code></pre>
</div>
<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>Simple - x and y are not <code>lists</code>, they are just references to list. there is some point in memory containing list data and both x and y just know where it is. So when you do <code>y = x</code> and change list referenced by y, this change will be reflected at <code>print(x)</code>.</p>
</div>
