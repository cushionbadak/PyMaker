<p>I have two tuples <code>a = (('1',), ('2',))</code>
and <code>b = (('3',), ('4',))</code></p>
<p>I need an output like</p>
<pre><code>result = (('1',3',), ('2','4',))
</code></pre>
<p>I have tried converting the tuples into a list and used <code>zip</code> to merge into a single tuple <code>tuple(zip(list(a), list(b)))</code> and also <code>tuple(zip(a,b))</code> which yields <code>((('1',), ('3',)), (('2',), ('4',)))</code> as the result. </p>
<p>What should I be doing to get the desired result? I saw about immutability of tuples but I am getting the tuples from another service and I all I can do is to convert the obtained tuple to a list to get the expected output.</p>

<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>Zipping is the right approach, but you then have to flatten your paired tuples still; you could concatenate them:</p>
<pre><code>result = tuple(x + y for x, y in zip(a, b))
</code></pre>
<p>Alternatively, flatten your <code>a</code> and <code>b</code> tuples <em>before</em> zipping:</p>
<pre><code>result = tuple(zip((v for t in a for v in t), (v for t in b for v in t)))
</code></pre>
<p>Flattening can also be done with <a href="https://docs.python.org/3/library/itertools.html#itertools.chain" rel="nofollow noreferrer"><code>itertools.chain()</code></a>, which is perhaps more readable:</p>
<pre><code>from itertools import chain

result = tuple(zip(chain(*a), chain(*b)))
</code></pre>
<p>Demo:</p>
<pre><code>&gt;&gt;&gt; a = (('1',), ('2',))
&gt;&gt;&gt; b = (('3',), ('4',))
&gt;&gt;&gt; tuple(x + y for x, y in zip(a, b))
(('1', '3'), ('2', '4'))
&gt;&gt;&gt; tuple(zip((v for t in a for v in t), (v for t in b for v in t)))
(('1', '3'), ('2', '4'))
&gt;&gt;&gt; from itertools import chain
&gt;&gt;&gt; tuple(zip(chain(*a), chain(*b)))
(('1', '3'), ('2', '4'))
</code></pre>
