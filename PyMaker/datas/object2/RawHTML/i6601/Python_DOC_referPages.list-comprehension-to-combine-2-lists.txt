<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>I have two sequence as below</p>
<pre><code>t = [[1,2,3],[4,5,6],[7,8,9]]
alpha = ['a','b','c']
</code></pre>
<p>How to use list comprehension to modify t as below:-</p>
<pre><code>t = [[1,2,3,'a'],[4,5,6,'b'],[7,8,9,'c']]
</code></pre>
</div>
<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>Iterate over the indices:</p>
<pre><code>t = [[1,2,3],[4,5,6],[7,8,9]]
alpha = ['a','b','c']
print([t[i] + [alpha[i]] for i in range(len(alpha))])
</code></pre>
</div>
<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>You could use <code>zip</code> to get corresponding pairs of elements, and from there it's just a matter of joining:</p>
<pre><code>&gt;&gt;&gt; t = [[1,2,3],[4,5,6],[7,8,9]]
&gt;&gt;&gt; alpha = ['a','b','c']
&gt;&gt;&gt; [x[0] + [x[1]] for x in zip(t,alpha)]
[[1, 2, 3, 'a'], [4, 5, 6, 'b'], [7, 8, 9, 'c']]
</code></pre>
</div>
<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>use <a href="https://docs.python.org/3/library/functions.html#zip" rel="nofollow noreferrer">zip</a>:</p>
<pre><code>t = [[1,2,3],[4,5,6],[7,8,9]]
alpha = ['a','b','c']
new_list = [a+[b] for a, b in zip(t, alpha)]
</code></pre>
<p>Output:</p>
<pre><code>[[1, 2, 3, 'a'], [4, 5, 6, 'b'], [7, 8, 9, 'c']]
</code></pre>
</div>
<span class="comment-copy">This appears to be the most pythonic way to do it. This is a clear list comprehension that can be used in other situations that require two lists to be iterated over and manipulated in some way.</span>
