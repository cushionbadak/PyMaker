<p>I have photo files in arc map with file names such as:
<code>image_name = (1-3#s)_(10#'s).jpg</code>. For example:</p>
<pre><code>1_1z1z1z1z1z.jpg
599_iiiiiiiiii.jpg
</code></pre>
<p>I want to remove the 6 characters that follow the underscore (<code>_</code>), and remove (<code>.jpg</code>). For the example, the result would be:</p>
<pre><code>1_1z1z
599_iiii
</code></pre>
<p><code>image_name.strip('jpg')</code> removes the file extension as a simple expression, but I can't figure out how to make a combined string command in the advanced section to accommodate the second rule as well.</p>
</div>
<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>You can use <a href="https://docs.python.org/3/library/re.html#re.sub" rel="nofollow noreferrer"><code>re.sub</code></a> to match the first 6 characters (any 6 characters <code>.{6}</code>) after the <code>_</code> and remove them all. Then also remove the fixed <code>.jpg</code> from the result:</p>
<pre><code>&gt;&gt;&gt; name = '123_1a2b3c4d5e.jpg'
&gt;&gt;&gt; re.sub('_.{6}', '_', name).replace('.jpg', '')
'123_4d5e'
</code></pre>
<p>Note that <code>str.strip('jpg')</code> is <em>not</em> a safe (or correct) way to strip the <code>jpg</code> extension. <a href="https://docs.python.org/3/library/stdtypes.html#str.strip" rel="nofollow noreferrer"><code>str.strip</code></a> will remove <em>all leading and trailing instances</em> of the characters given, for example:</p>
<pre><code>&gt;&gt;&gt; 'jppj-test-jpg'.strip('jpg')
'-test-'
</code></pre>
