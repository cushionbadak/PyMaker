<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p><a href="https://docs.python.org/3/library/re.html#re.finditer" rel="nofollow noreferrer"><code>re.finditer</code></a> accepts regex pattern, and <a href="https://docs.python.org/3/library/re.html#regular-expression-syntax" rel="nofollow noreferrer">parentheses have special meaning</a> in regex (they form groups). If you want to match literal <code>(</code> and <code>)</code> you have be escape them (like <code>\(</code> and <code>\)</code>), or enclose them in character class (like <code>[(]</code> and <code>[)]</code>).</p>
<pre><code>import re
kwSe='\('
kwEe='\)'
kwS=[match.start() for match in re.finditer(kwSe,str(locationString))]
kwE=[match.start() for match in re.finditer(kwEe,str(locationString))]
</code></pre>
