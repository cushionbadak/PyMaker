<p>I want to print"After tax, your total is: $8.74999125."
no whitespace after $ sign. </p>
<p>how could I do this in this statement? </p>
<pre><code>print("After tax, your total is: $",total_price). 
</code></pre>
<p>The output of this statement adds whitespace after $.</p>
<p>total price is type float. </p>
</div>
<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>Convert <code>total_price</code> to string, then use string concatenations: </p>
<pre><code>print("After tax, your total is: $" + str(total_price))
</code></pre>
<p>Or use <a href="https://docs.python.org/3.5/library/string.html#format-string-syntax" rel="nofollow">strings formatting</a>:</p>
<pre><code>print("After tax, your total is: ${}".format(total_price))
</code></pre>
</div>
<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>I would use <a href="https://docs.python.org/3/library/string.html#format-string-syntax" rel="nofollow">string formatting</a></p>
<pre><code>print("After tax, your total is: ${}".format(total_price))
</code></pre>
</div>
<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>Third variant: it is also possible to change separator symbol (to empty string in current case):</p>
<pre><code>print("After tax, your total is: $",total_price, sep=''). 
</code></pre>
</div>
