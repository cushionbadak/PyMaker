<p>I'm reading through <em>Natural Language Processing With Python</em> and I've come across:</p>
<pre><code>count, total = 3205, 9375
"accuracy for %d words: %2.4f%%" % (total, 100 * count / total)
</code></pre>
<p>I've seen the <code>%.4f</code>but never with an integer in front of the decimal point. Playing around with it the following all print the same thing (<code>34.1867</code>):</p>
<pre><code>print('%.4f' %(100*count/total))
print('%0.4f' %(100*count/total))
print('%1.4f' %(100*count/total))
print('%2.4f' %(100*count/total))
</code></pre>
<p>etcetera. What does the value in front of the decimal do?  </p>

<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>The first number specifies the <a href="https://docs.python.org/3.6/library/string.html#format-specification-mini-language" rel="nofollow noreferrer">minimum field width</a>, i.e. the minimum length of the complete output. </p>
<p>For example, to force the output to have at least 6 characters with 2 after the decimal point you do:</p>
<pre><code>'%06.2f' % (3.141592653589793,)
</code></pre>
<p>Output:</p>
<pre><code>003.14
</code></pre>
</div>
