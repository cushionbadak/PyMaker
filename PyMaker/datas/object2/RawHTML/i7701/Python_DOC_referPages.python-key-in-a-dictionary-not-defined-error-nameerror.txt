<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>You are getting the error you are seeing, because what you are doing here: </p>
<p><code>Dict = {"a","b","c","d"}</code></p>
<p>is creating a <a href="https://docs.python.org/3/tutorial/datastructures.html#sets" rel="nofollow noreferrer">set</a> and not a <a href="https://docs.python.org/3/tutorial/datastructures.html#dictionaries" rel="nofollow noreferrer">dictionary</a>. Even though they share the <em>curly braces</em>, does not mean they are both one and the same. </p>
<p>if you do a <code>print(type(Dict))</code>, you will see you in fact have a <code>set</code> and not a <code>dict</code>:</p>
<pre><code>&gt;&gt;&gt; Dict = {"a","b","c","d"}
&gt;&gt;&gt; print(type(Dict))
&lt;type 'set'&gt;
</code></pre>
<p>You seem to be looking for <a href="https://docs.python.org/3/library/collections.html#collections.defaultdict" rel="nofollow noreferrer">defaultdict</a>, that takes a <code>list</code> as its <em>default</em> value structure: </p>
<pre><code>&gt;&gt;&gt; from collections import defaultdict
&gt;&gt;&gt; your_dict = defaultdict(list)
&gt;&gt;&gt; your_dict['a'].append(1)
&gt;&gt;&gt; print(your_dict)
defaultdict(&lt;type 'list'&gt;, {'a': [1]})
&gt;&gt;&gt; print(your_dict['a'])
[1]
</code></pre>
</div>
