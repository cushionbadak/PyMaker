<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>I have a list with multiple words per index, and i need to split the words into different indexes.</p>
<pre><code>mylist = ['hell o', 'geg gg' etc...]
</code></pre>
<p>How do i turn the above list into.</p>
<pre><code>newList = ['hell', 'o' , 'geg', 'gg']
</code></pre>
</div>
<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>You could use a <a href="https://docs.python.org/3/tutorial/datastructures.html#list-comprehensions" rel="nofollow">list comprehension</a>:</p>
<pre><code>In [192]: mylist = ['hell o', 'geg gg']

In [193]: [part for item in mylist for part in item.split()]
Out[193]: ['hell', 'o', 'geg', 'gg']
</code></pre>
<hr/>
<pre><code>[part for item in mylist for part in item.split()]
</code></pre>
<p>is equivalent to to the value assigned to <code>result</code> by</p>
<pre><code>result = list()
for item in mylist:
    for part in item.split():
        result.append(part)
</code></pre>
</div>
<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>Another way of doing it</p>
<pre><code>&gt;&gt;&gt; from itertools import chain
&gt;&gt;&gt; mylist = ['hell o', 'geg gg']
&gt;&gt;&gt; list(chain.from_iterable(map(str.split, mylist)))
['hell', 'o', 'geg', 'gg']
</code></pre>
