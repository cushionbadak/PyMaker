<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>I don't really understand the .strip function.</p>
<p>Say I have a string</p>
<pre><code>xxx = 'hello, world'
</code></pre>
<p>and I want to strip the comma.
Why doesn't</p>
<pre><code>print xxx.strip(',')
</code></pre>
<p>work?</p>
</div>
<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p><code>str.strip()</code> removes characters from the <em>start and end of a string only</em>. From the <a href="https://docs.python.org/2/library/stdtypes.html#str.strip" rel="noreferrer"><code>str.strip()</code> documentation</a>:</p>
<blockquote>
<p>Return a copy of the string with the <strong>leading and trailing characters</strong> removed. </p>
</blockquote>
<p>Emphasis mine.</p>
<p>Use <a href="https://docs.python.org/2/library/stdtypes.html#str.replace" rel="noreferrer"><code>str.replace()</code></a> to remove text from everywhere in the string:</p>
<pre><code>xxx.replace(',', '')
</code></pre>
<p>For a <em>set</em> of characters use a regular expression:</p>
<pre><code>import re

re.sub(r'[,!?]', '', xxx)
</code></pre>
<p>Demo:</p>
<pre><code>&gt;&gt;&gt; xxx = 'hello, world'
&gt;&gt;&gt; xxx.replace(',', '')
'hello world'
</code></pre>
</div>
<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p><a href="https://docs.python.org/3/library/stdtypes.html#str.strip"><code>str.strip</code></a> removes characters from the beginning or end of the string, not in the middle.</p>
<pre><code>&gt;&gt;&gt; ',hello, world,'.strip(',')
'hello, world'
</code></pre>
<p>If you want remove character from everywhere, you should use <a href="https://docs.python.org/3/library/stdtypes.html#str.replace"><code>str.replace</code></a> instead:</p>
<pre><code>&gt;&gt;&gt; 'hello, world'.replace(',', '')
'hello world'
</code></pre>
</div>
<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>You can also use the <strong>translate</strong> method of the <em>string</em> class.  If you pass in <strong>None</strong> for the table parameter, then only the character deletion step is executed.</p>
<pre><code>&gt;&gt;&gt; 'hello, world'.translate(None,',')
'hello world'
</code></pre>
</div>
