<p>You can do it like this:</p>
<pre><code>import re

text = """iso.3.6.1.4.1.25355.3.2.6.4.2.5.1.7.4.2.13.36 = Hex-STRING: 84 16 F9 B0 05 1F 70 4F 57 4C A8 1F 60 E3 27 A1
E0 CD C4 E9 84 C3 68 53 C4 E9 84 C9 7A BB C4 E9 84 AC
C3 68 53 27 CC 58 97 84 AC 74 F7 60 E3 27 CC 58 97
84 AC 74 F7 60 E3 27 CC 58 97 84 AC 74 F7 60 E3 27
CC 58 97
84 AC 74 F7 60 E3 27 C3 68 53 84 AC 74 F7 60 E3 27 CC
58 97"""

result = re.search("Hex-STRING:\s(.+)$", text, re.DOTALL)
print(result.group())
</code></pre>
<p><strong>Output</strong></p>
<pre><code>Hex-STRING: 84 16 F9 B0 05 1F 70 4F 57 4C A8 1F 60 E3 27 A1
E0 CD C4 E9 84 C3 68 53 C4 E9 84 C9 7A BB C4 E9 84 AC
C3 68 53 27 CC 58 97 84 AC 74 F7 60 E3 27 CC 58 97
84 AC 74 F7 60 E3 27 CC 58 97 84 AC 74 F7 60 E3 27
CC 58 97
84 AC 74 F7 60 E3 27 C3 68 53 84 AC 74 F7 60 E3 27 CC
58 97
</code></pre>
<p>From the <a href="https://docs.python.org/3/library/re.html" rel="nofollow noreferrer">documentation</a>:</p>
<blockquote>
<p>If the DOTALL flag has been specified, this matches any character
  including a newline.</p>
