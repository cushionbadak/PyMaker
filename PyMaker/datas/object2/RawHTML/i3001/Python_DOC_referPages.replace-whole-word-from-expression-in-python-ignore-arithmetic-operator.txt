<p>I have an expression as string like"(addtwo<em>decimal</em>p_cost)/(cost+density)".
I want to replace cost only but but its replacing p_cost as well like -
p_"replaced value" . I have tried word boundaries but no luck please help. </p>
<pre><code>expr = '(addtwo*decimal*p_cost)/(cost+density)'

expr = string.replace(expr, r"\b%s\b" % str(' cost '), str(3)) not doing anything.
</code></pre>

<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>Using regex.</p>
<pre><code>import re
expr = '(addtwo*decimal*p_cost)/(cost+density)'
print(re.sub(r"\bcost\b", "3", expr))
</code></pre>
<p><strong>Output:</strong></p>
<pre><code>(addtwo*decimal*p_cost)/(3+density)
</code></pre>
</div>
<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<pre><code>import re

expr = r'(addtwo*decimal*p_cost)/(cost+density)'

expr2 = re.sub("(?&lt;![_])cost(?![_])","COST", expr)
print(expr2)
</code></pre>
<p>Insert all characters inhibiting the replacement of search word in the square brackets before (<code>(?&lt;![_])</code>) and after (<code>(?![_])</code>) of the search word (<code>cost</code> in this case) in the regex pattern string.</p>
</div>
<span class="comment-copy">If you want to do a regular expression substitution, use <a href="https://docs.python.org/3/library/re.html#re.sub" rel="nofollow noreferrer"><code>re.sub</code></a>.</span>
