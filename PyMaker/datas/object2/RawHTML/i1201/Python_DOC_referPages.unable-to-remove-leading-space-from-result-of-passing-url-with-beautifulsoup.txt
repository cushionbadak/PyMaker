<p>I am dealing with a dictionary that contains a lot of HTML links with incomplete urls, in the form:</p>
<pre><code>&lt;li&gt;&lt;b&gt;Random Thing&lt;/b&gt;: &lt;a href="dl_img/CM2233.jpg" target=_blank&gt;JPG&lt;/a&gt;&lt;/li&gt;
</code></pre>
<p>I am using BeautifulSoup to extract just the URL, and append it to the domain to have a complete URL. BeautifulSoup works well, but the string returned has a space at the start of the link. I am trying to use lstrip to remove this, but it has no effect.</p>
<p>I am using the following code:</p>
<pre><code>for datadict in temp:

</code></pre>
<p>Which outputs the following:</p>
<pre><code>www.example.com/media/ dl_img/CM2233.jpg
</code></pre>
<p>What other techniques can I use to remove the whitespace at the beginning of what BeautifulSoup returns?</p>

<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>The space you see is just something <code>print()</code> uses as a default delimiter when multiple arguments are passed into it. And you could change this separator if needed:</p>
<pre><code>print("www.example.com/media/", a['href'], sep='')
</code></pre>
<p>In general though, you could and should use <a href="https://docs.python.org/3/library/urllib.parse.html#urllib.parse.urljoin" rel="nofollow noreferrer"><code>urllib.parse.urljoin()</code></a> to join parts of a URL:</p>
<pre><code>from urllib.parse import urljoin

base_url = "www.example.com/media/"

for a in soup.find_all('a', href=True):
    print(urljoin(base_url, a['href']))
</code></pre>
