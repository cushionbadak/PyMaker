<pre><code>sum = sqrt((s1 ** 2) + (s2 ** 2) + (2 * s1 * s2 * cos(angle)))
print(sum)
</code></pre>
<p><code># Where s1 and s2 are the sizes of the vectors, respectively.</code></p>
<p>But the, the cos in the equation returned a weird value (the angle was 60, so the <code>cos(angle)</code> should be 1/2, right?).</p>
<p>Also, I tried changing the <code>cos</code> with <code>acos</code> after reading other solutions, but it returned <code>ValueError: math domain error</code>.</p>

<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>Python's trigonometric functions use radians, rather than degrees.  Fortunately, the <code>math</code> module includes a function to perform the conversion for you:</p>
<pre><code>from math import cos, radians

sum = sqrt((s1 ** 2) + (s2 ** 2) + (2 * s1 * s2 * cos(radians(angle))))
print(sum)
</code></pre>
</div>
<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>Argument of cos() should be expressed in radians.</p>
