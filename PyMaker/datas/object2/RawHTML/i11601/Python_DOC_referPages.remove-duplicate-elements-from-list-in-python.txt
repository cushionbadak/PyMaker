<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>You can't use <code>list</code> type elements in a <code>set</code>, since <code>list</code> is mutable entity. For the same reason, you can't use <code>list</code> as key of a dictionary. You need to have an immutable type, like <code>tuple</code>.</p>
<p>So, you can convert inner elements to tuple before passing to set:</p>
<pre><code>set(tuple(li) for li in l)
</code></pre>
<p>Check <a href="https://docs.python.org/3/tutorial/datastructures.html#tuples-and-sequences" rel="nofollow">this section to doc</a>:</p>
<blockquote>
<p>Though tuples may seem similar to lists, they are often used in
  different situations and for different purposes. Tuples are immutable,
  and usually contain a heterogeneous sequence of elements that are
  accessed via unpacking (see later in this section) or indexing (or
  even by attribute in the case of namedtuples). Lists are mutable, and
  their elements are usually homogeneous and are accessed by iterating
  over the list.</p>
</blockquote>
