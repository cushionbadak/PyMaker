<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>The issue is that <code>del result[b]</code> changes the composition (and the length of) <code>result</code>, thereby interfering with your loop.</p>
<p>Perhaps the easiest way to fix this is by rephrasing your code as a list comprehension:</p>
<pre><code>result = [r for r in result if 'FillLevel' not in r[2:3]]
</code></pre>
<p>Alternatively, you could fix it by iterating in reverse:</p>
<pre><code>for b in range(len(result) - 1, -1, -1):
    if 'FillLevel' in result[b][2:3]:
        del result[b]
</code></pre>
</div>
<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>Let's say there are 10 items in the list.</p>
<p>Half-way through you delete one of the items; now there are 9 items in the list.</p>
<p>In the last cycle, your loop asks for the tenth item. My guess is that's where the index error is happening (though it could be due to the <code>[2:3]</code> call as well, depending on the contents of your list)</p>
<p>A more pythonic solution would be</p>
<pre><code>result = [val for val in result if 'FillLevel' not in val[2:3]]
</code></pre>
</div>

<pre><code>b = 0
while b &lt; len(result) :
    if 'FillLevel' in result[b][2:3]:

    b += 1 
</code></pre>
