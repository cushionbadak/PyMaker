<p>I have a list of words that I want to reorder based on the frequency of its occurrence as a substring in another list of strings:</p>
<pre><code>list_to_reorder=['apm', 'mba', 'bac', 'ms', 'grad']
list_with_strings=['don.mba', 'kun.ms', 'bob.mba', 'liz.grad', 'lily.apm', 'tina.mba', 'sash.ms', 'gin.grad', 'moma.bs']
</code></pre>
<p>So if I look at the count of occurrences of each element in list_to_reorder, I get:</p>
<pre><code>mba =&gt; 3
ms  =&gt; 2
grad=&gt; 2
apm =&gt; 1
bs  =&gt; 1
</code></pre>
<p>and then would like to reorder the first list like this which is the expected result:</p>
<pre><code>['mba', 'ms', 'grad', 'apm', 'bac']
</code></pre>
<p>Thanks!</p>
</div>
<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>Using <a href="https://docs.python.org/2/library/collections.html#collections.Counter" rel="nofollow noreferrer"><code>collections.Counter</code></a> and <a href="https://docs.python.org/3/library/functions.html#sorted" rel="nofollow noreferrer"><code>sorted</code></a> </p>
<pre><code>from collections import Counter

&gt;&gt;&gt; d = Counter([x.split('.')[-1] for x in list_with_strings])
&gt;&gt;&gt; sorted(list_to_reorder, key= lambda x: d[x], reverse=True)
['mba', 'ms', 'grad', 'apm', 'bac']
</code></pre>
<p>Can also use <a href="https://docs.python.org/3/reference/datamodel.html#object.__getitem__" rel="nofollow noreferrer"><code>d.__getitem__</code></a> as the <code>key</code>, but I'd say it gets less intuitive</p>
