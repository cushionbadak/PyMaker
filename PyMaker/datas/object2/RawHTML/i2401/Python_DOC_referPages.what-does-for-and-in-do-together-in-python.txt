<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p><code>for a in b</code> <strong>iterates</strong> through <code>b</code> by calling <code>iter(b)</code> first to get an iterator of <code>b</code>, and repeatedly calling <code>next()</code> on the iterator and assigning the values to <code>a</code> before running the loop body.</p>
<p>An iterator of a python dictionary generates all its <strong>keys</strong>, so you're getting all keys of <code>thisdict</code> with <code>x</code> in your loop and printing them.</p>
</div>
<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>The <a href="https://www.w3schools.com/python/python_for_loops.asp" rel="nofollow noreferrer">for loop</a> loops through the keys in the <a href="https://docs.python.org/3/tutorial/datastructures.html" rel="nofollow noreferrer">dictionary</a>. To loop through and get the values of each key you could do (<a href="https://repl.it/repls/WornFrighteningLamp" rel="nofollow noreferrer">Try it online</a>):</p>
<pre><code>for x in thisdict:
   print(thisdict[x])
</code></pre>
<p>and it would output:</p>
<pre><code>Ford
Mustang
2018
</code></pre>
