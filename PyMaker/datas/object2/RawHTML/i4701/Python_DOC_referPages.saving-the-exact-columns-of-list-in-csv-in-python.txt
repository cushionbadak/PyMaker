<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>You are almost there:</p>
<pre><code>from collections import OrderedDict
import csv

listOfDicts = [OrderedDict([('id', '1'),
              ('name', 'Jack'),
              ('email', 'jack@email.com')]),
              OrderedDict([('id', '2'),
              ('name', 'Ricky'),
              ('email', 'ricky@email.com')])]
path='someFilename.csv'
fields=['Name','Email']

with open(path, 'w', newline="", encoding="utf8") as f:   
    writer = csv.writer(f)
    writer.writerow(fields)
    for d in listOfDicts:  # d are your different dictionaries 
        writer.writerow([d['name'], d['email']])
</code></pre>
<p>creates the file :</p>
<pre><code>Name,Email
Jack,jack@email.com
Ricky,ricky@email.com
</code></pre>
<p>It is important to <code>open(..)</code> the file with <code>newline=""</code> so you do not get additional newlines into it - <code>csv</code> will handle those on its own - you also should specifiy the <code>encoding</code> - just to make sure.</p>
<p>I fixed some other indentation and minor parenthesis errors on your example data on the way and provided the needed imports to get a working example.</p>
<p><a href="https://docs.python.org/3/library/csv.html#csv.writer" rel="nofollow noreferrer">csv-writer</a></p>
