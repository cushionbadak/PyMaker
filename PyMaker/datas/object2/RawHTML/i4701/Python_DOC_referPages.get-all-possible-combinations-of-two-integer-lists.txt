<p>I have two lists of integers. I want to make all possible combinations of elements in list-1 with elements with list-2. For Example:</p>
<pre><code>List-1    List-2
1         5
2         6
</code></pre>
<p>I need another list of all possible combinations like:</p>
<pre><code>element-1    element-2
1            5
1            6
2            5
2            6
</code></pre>
<p>How to do that in python?</p>
</div>
<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>You are looking for <a href="https://docs.python.org/3/library/itertools.html#itertools.product" rel="nofollow noreferrer"><code>itertools.product()</code></a>:</p>
<pre><code>&gt;&gt;&gt; import itertools
&gt;&gt;&gt; list(itertools.product([1, 2], [5, 6]))
[(1, 5), (1, 6), (2, 5), (2, 6)]
</code></pre>
</div>
<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>You can try itertools :</p>
<pre><code>list_1=[1,2]
list_2=[5,6]

import itertools
print([i for i in itertools.product(list_1,list_2)])
</code></pre>
<p>output:</p>
<pre><code>[(1, 5), (1, 6), (2, 5), (2, 6)]
</code></pre>
</div>
<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>Size of list1 is always bigger than or equal to list2.</p>
<pre><code>import itertools
list1=[1, 2]
list2=[5, 6]

[zip(x,list2) for x in itertools.permutations(list1,len(list2))]
</code></pre>
