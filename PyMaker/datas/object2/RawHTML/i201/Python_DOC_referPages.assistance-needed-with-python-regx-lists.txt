<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>Assuming your original data looks like this:</p>
<pre><code>data = ['First Name1', 'Last Name1', 'First Name2', 'Last Name2','First Name3', 'Last Name3']
</code></pre>
<p>You can just retrieve every other item:</p>
<pre><code>x = [[data[i], data[i + 1]] for i in range(0, len(data), 2)]
</code></pre>
<p>And the result will be a nested list that you can retrieve data from:</p>
<pre><code>x -&gt; [['First Name1', 'Last Name1'], ['First Name2', 'Last Name2'], ['First Name3', 'Last Name3']]

x[0] -&gt; ['First Name1', 'Last Name1']
</code></pre>
</div>
<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>It's not easy to understand what exactly it is you're trying to achieve. There is no such thing as a 'vertical list' in Python. </p>
<p>Do you want to group every <code>First NameX</code> with <code>Last NameX</code>? In that case, you could make <a href="https://docs.python.org/3/tutorial/datastructures.html#tuples-and-sequences" rel="nofollow noreferrer"><code>tuples</code></a> out of the pairs.</p>
<p>Do you want to print your list to the console while grouping every <code>First NameX</code> with <code>Last NameX</code>? In that case, it's still easier to make tuples out of them, and print them like this:</p>
<pre class="lang-py prettyprint-override"><code>my_list = [('First Name1', 'Last Name1'), ('First Name2', 'Last Name2'), ('First Name3', 'Last Name3')]

for elem in my_list:
    print(elem[0], elem[1])
</code></pre>
</div>
