<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>In <code>print str((23-11)/a*b)</code> this is what happens</p>
<p>1.Calculate 23-11</p>
<p>2.Divide step by a(0)</p>
<p>3.Multiply step 2 by b(5)</p>
<p>It is step 2 which gives the zero division error</p>
<p>However in <code>print str((23-11)/b*a)</code> this is what happens</p>
<p>1.Calculate 23-11</p>
<p>2.Divide step 2 by 5</p>
<p>3.Multiply step 3 by 0.</p>
<p>There is no ZeroDivisonError because the computer divides by 5 then multiplies by 0, not divides by 0*5. This is because in order of operations Multiplication and Division are of equal priority so they are just executed left to right.</p>
</div>
<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>The evaluation of <code>(23-11)/a*b</code> is decomposed as follow: ((23 - 11) / a) * b</p>
<p>See: <a href="https://docs.python.org/3/reference/expressions.html#operator-precedence" rel="nofollow">Operator precedence</a></p>
</div>
