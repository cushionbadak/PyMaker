<p>I want to filter a string in Python, to get only commas <code>,</code> and numbers <code>[0-9]</code>.</p>
<pre><code>import re
x="$HGHG54646JHGJH,54546654"
m=re.sub("[^0-9]","",x)
print(m)
</code></pre>
<p>The result is:</p>
<pre><code>5464654546654
</code></pre>
<p>instead of:</p>
<pre><code>54646,54546654
</code></pre>
</div>
<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>With your current code, you simply match <code>[0-9]</code>. Simply add a comma <code>,</code> as a valid character, and use a backslash to escape to the literal (<code>\,</code>):</p>
<pre><code>import re
x="$HGHG54646JHGJH,54546654"
m=re.sub("[^0-9\,]","",x)
print(m)
</code></pre>
<p><strong>Outputs:</strong></p>
<pre><code>54646,54546654
</code></pre>
<p><a href="https://docs.python.org/3/library/re.html#regular-expression-syntax" rel="nofollow">The docs</a> have further information regarding other special characters that must be escaped with a backslash to acquire the literal, such as <code>?</code> and <code>*</code>.</p>
