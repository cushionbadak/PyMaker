<p>The following line of code prints the standard deviation and mean on my graph using matplotlib in python. My question is. How do I convert the values to 2 decimals?</p>
<pre><code>ax.text(60, 0.01, r'$\mu={},\ \sigma={}$'.format(np.mean(stat_input),np.std(stat_input)))
</code></pre>
</div>
<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<pre><code>&gt;&gt;&gt; '{:.2f}'.format(123.456789)
'123.46'
</code></pre>
<p>For details see: <a href="https://docs.python.org/3/library/string.html#format-string-syntax" rel="nofollow noreferrer">Format String Syntax</a></p>
<p>So, in your case the line becomes:</p>
<pre><code>ax.text(60, 0.01, r'$\mu={:.2f},\ \sigma={:.2f}$'.format(
    np.mean(stat_input), np.std(stat_input)
))
</code></pre>
</div>
