<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<pre><code>"something".join(some_iterable)
</code></pre>
<p>will take each element from <code>some_iterable</code> and put  in between. In your case</p>
<pre><code>"\n\n".join("hello world")
</code></pre>
<p>takes each element from the string "hello world" (i.e. each character) and puts \n\n in between. So you get each character and then two newlines after it.</p>
<p>You don't need the <code>join</code> in your case. just do </p>
<pre><code>test3=f'{test1} {test2}\n\n'
</code></pre>
</div>
<span class="comment-copy">because <code>join</code> treats its arguments as iterables. iterating over strings produces the individual letters which in your case are <i>joined</i> using two new line characters (<code>/n/n</code>)</span>
