<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p><a href="https://docs.python.org/3/library/collections.html#ordereddict-objects" rel="nofollow"><code>OrderdDict</code></a> are <code>dict</code>s. Members are access by key, not by positon:</p>
<pre><code>stage = OrderedDict([('a', '5678'), ('b', '56')])
print(stage["a"])
stage["a"] = 'changed'
print(stage)
</code></pre>
<p>Output:</p>
<pre><code>'5678'
OrderedDict([('a', 'changed'), ('b', '56')])
</code></pre>
</div>
<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>To answer your question, the best is to look at the exact code source for the <code>items</code> method of the OrderedDict:</p>
<pre><code>def items(self):
        'od.items() -&gt; list of (key, value) pairs in od'
        return [(key, self[key]) for key in self]
</code></pre>
<p>So reading that, it is very clear why changing an element of item will not change your dictionary. As I had assumed above, the function you are calling does not return a data memeber directly, but it rewrites the values of your dictionary in a list and returns that list.</p>
<p>Note that you can do the following:</p>
<pre><code>d = OrderedDict({'a':[0]})
d.items()[0][1].append(1)
</code></pre>
<p>This will actually modify your list.</p>
<p>In your specific example, if you want to modify your dictionary, you can do the following:</p>
<pre><code>d[d.keys()[0]] = 3
</code></pre>
