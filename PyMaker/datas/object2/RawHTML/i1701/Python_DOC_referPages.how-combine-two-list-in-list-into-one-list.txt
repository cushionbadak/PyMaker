<p>Let's say I have:</p>
<pre><code>A = [[a,b,c,d],[1,2,3,4]]
B = [[1.2,1.7],[1.6,1.8]]
</code></pre>
<p>I want to combine 2 list to one list</p>
<pre><code>C = [[a,b,c,d,1.2,1.7],[1,2,3,4,1.6,1.8]]
</code></pre>
<p>How can I do that in Python?
Thanks</p>

<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>There are lot's of different ways of combining two lists. </p>
<p>You can add two lists together:</p>
<pre><code>[1,2,3] + [4,5,6]
</code></pre>
<p>Will give you:</p>
<pre><code>[1,2,3,4,5,6]
</code></pre>
<p>You can append a list to a list of lists:</p>
<pre><code>[[1,2,3],[4,5,6]].append([7,8,9])
</code></pre>
<p>Will give you:</p>
<pre><code>[[1,2,3],[4,5,6],[7,8,9]]
</code></pre>
<p>You can zip two lists as well:</p>
<pre><code>zip([1,2,3],[4,5,6])
</code></pre>
<p>Will give you:</p>
<pre><code>[(1,4),(2,5),(3,6)]
</code></pre>
<p>What it looks like you're looking for is:</p>
<pre><code>C = []

for sublist_1, sublist_2 in zip(A, B):
    C.append(sublist_1 + sublist_2)
</code></pre>
<p>Or more compactly using list comprehension:</p>
<pre><code>C = [sublist_1 + sublist_2 for sublist_2, sublist_2 in zip(A,B)]
</code></pre>
</div>
<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>You can use <code>zip</code> and  <a href="https://docs.python.org/3/library/itertools.html#itertools.starmap" rel="nofollow noreferrer"><code>itertools.starmap</code></a> for this</p>
<pre><code>In [110]: A
Out[110]: [[2, 4, 5, 6], [1, 2, 3, 4]]

In [111]: B
Out[111]: [[1.2, 1.7], [1.6, 1.8]]

In [112]: import itertools

In [113]: list(itertools.starmap(lambda x,y:x+y,zip(A,B)))
Out[113]: [[2, 4, 5, 6, 1.2, 1.7], [1, 2, 3, 4, 1.6, 1.8]]
</code></pre>
</div>

<pre><code>for i in range(len(a)):
    a[i].extend(b[i])
    c.append(a[i])
