<p>How can I split a single .txt file into two or more .txt files when a white line occours?</p>
<p>Here is a example of what my txt looks like:</p>
<pre><code>a s d d d d s d f
f d e s s a d f s
a s d d d d s d f
f d e s s a d f s

dsdesd
dseesdse
</code></pre>
<p>I would like to know how to split this single text file into:</p>
<p>First txt file:</p>
<pre><code>a s d d d d s d f
f d e s s a d f s
a s d d d d s d f
f d e s s a d f s
</code></pre>
<p>Second txt file:</p>
<pre><code>dsdesd
dseesdse
</code></pre>

<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>If you know the file will only have one blank line, you can <a href="https://docs.python.org/3/library/stdtypes.html#str.split" rel="nofollow noreferrer"><strong><code>split</code></strong></a> the contents at the double newline character:</p>
<pre><code>with open('input.txt') as f:
    contents = f.read()

output1, output2 = contents.split('\n\n')

with open('output1.txt', 'w') as o1:
    o1.write(output1)

with open('output2.txt', 'w') as o2:
    o2.write(output2)
</code></pre>
<p>If your file has more than one blank line this will fail as the split will return more than 2 parts and try to assign them to only two names, <code>output1</code> and <code>output2</code>. <a href="https://docs.python.org/3/library/stdtypes.html#str.split" rel="nofollow noreferrer"><strong><code>split</code></strong></a> can be told to only split a maximum amount of times so it may be safer to say:</p>
<pre><code>output1, output2 = contents.split('\n\n', 1)
</code></pre>
<p>If there are two or more blank lines, <code>output1</code> will be the contents up to the first blank line. <code>output2</code> will be everything after the first blank line, including any further blank lines.</p>
<p>Of course, this can fail if there are no blank lines.</p>
