<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>I think you should be doing: <code>print(str(Fraction(a, w)))</code>, not <code>print(str(Fraction(a/w)))</code>. What's happening here is you are getting the value of <code>a/w</code> and then trying to make it a Fraction, which results in the strange numbers you're getting. </p>
<p>Compare:</p>
<pre><code>&gt;&gt;&gt; Fraction(2/3)
Fraction(6004799503160661, 9007199254740992)
&gt;&gt;&gt; Fraction(2, 3)
Fraction(2, 3)
</code></pre>
</div>
<span class="comment-copy"><code>Fraction(a/w)</code> creates a fraction from the floating point approximation... Why not <code>Fraction(a, w)</code>? I'd recommend reading <a href="https://docs.python.org/3/library/fractions.html" rel="nofollow noreferrer">the docs</a>.</span>
