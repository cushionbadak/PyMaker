<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>Put the varying patterns inside a non-capturing group separated by <code>|</code> logical OR operator.</p>
<pre><code>(\w+)[\s\(](?:at|@)[\s\)](\w+)(?:\sdot\s(\w+)\sdot\sedu|\.(\w+)\.edu)
</code></pre>
<p>Add anchors if necessary.</p>
<pre><code>^(\w+)[\s\(](?:at|@)[\s\)](\w+)(?:\sdot\s(\w+)\sdot\s|\.(\w+)\.)edu$
</code></pre>
<p><a href="https://regex101.com/r/hK1uH1/6" rel="nofollow">DEMO</a></p>
</div>
<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p><code>[/at/@]</code> does not match what you think it does.  <code>[...]</code> denotes a character set, so that part is matching the individual characters <code>/</code>, <code>a</code>, <code>t</code>, or <code>@</code>.</p>
<p>In order to match either <code>at</code> or <code>@</code>, you will need to use:</p>
<pre><code>(?:at|@)
</code></pre>
<p>Here is a reference on Python's <a href="https://docs.python.org/3/library/re.html#regular-expression-syntax" rel="nofollow">Regular Expression Syntax</a>.</p>
