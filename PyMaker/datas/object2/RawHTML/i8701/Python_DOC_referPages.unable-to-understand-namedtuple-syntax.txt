<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>Because this:</p>
<pre><code>Person = namedtuple('Person', 'name age gender' 'address phone')
</code></pre>
<p>Is equivalent to this:</p>
<pre><code>Person = namedtuple('Person', 'name age genderaddress phone')
</code></pre>
<p><a href="https://docs.python.org/3/reference/lexical_analysis.html#string-literal-concatenation" rel="nofollow noreferrer">Here</a> is the documentation:</p>
<blockquote>
<p>Multiple adjacent string or bytes literals (delimited by whitespace),
  possibly using different quoting conventions, are allowed, and their
  meaning is the same as their concatenation. Thus, <code>"hello" 'world'</code> is
  equivalent to <code>"helloworld"</code>.</p>
</blockquote>
<p>So note, this is just part of the python syntax, and nothing special about <code>namedtuple</code>.</p>
<p>In the link, we have the following:</p>
<pre><code>ModelInput = namedtuple('ModelInput',
                        'enc_input dec_input target enc_len dec_len '
                        'origin_article origin_abstract')          ^
</code></pre>
<p>Notice the space.</p>
