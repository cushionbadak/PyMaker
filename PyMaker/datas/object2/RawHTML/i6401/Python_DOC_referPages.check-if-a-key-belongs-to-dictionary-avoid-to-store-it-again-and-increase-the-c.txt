<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>It's fine for your dictionary key to be a tuple (though in your post it's unclear why you want to dot his). All you need to do is make sure that you're checking for that tuple in your <code>if</code> statement:</p>
<pre><code>word_table = {}

for word in document.split():
    if (word,somevalue) in word_table:
        word_table[word, somevalue] += 1
    else:
        word_table[word, somevalue] = 1
</code></pre>
</div>
<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>The <a href="https://docs.python.org/3/library/collections.html#collections.Counter" rel="nofollow noreferrer"><code>collections.Counter</code></a> is probably what you are looking for:</p>
<pre><code>&gt;&gt;&gt; from collections import Counter
&gt;&gt;&gt; Counter("moon,moon,moon,sun".split(","))
Counter({'moon': 3, 'sun': 1})
</code></pre>
</div>
<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>I believe that the structure you need is the <a href="http://www.pythonforbeginners.com/collection/python-collections-counter" rel="nofollow noreferrer">counter</a> structure from the <strong>collections</strong> package.  You simply feed it your list of words, and you get a dictionary structure filled with the words and counts.</p>
</div>
<span class="comment-copy">An entry for <code>word, somevalue</code> is not an entry for <code>word</code>. <code>word in word_table</code> is checking for an entry for <code>word</code>.</span>
