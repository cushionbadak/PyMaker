<p>I have a list of strings (len 10000), some of them looks like</p>
<pre><code>Belin (*) 9
München 12
</code></pre>
<p>I want to strip away number and (*) at the end of string to get pure city name</p>
<pre><code>Berlin
München
</code></pre>
<p>can I use rstrip on this ?</p>
</div>
<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>Using regular expression function <a href="https://docs.python.org/3/library/re.html#re.sub" rel="nofollow"><code>re.sub</code></a> to remove (*) (<code>\(\*\)</code>) and digits (<code>\d+</code>) with extra leading spaces (<code>\s*</code>):</p>
<pre><code>&gt;&gt;&gt; cities = [
...     'Belin (*) 9',
...     'München 12',
...     'Los Angeles 9'
... ]
&gt;&gt;&gt; [re.sub('\s*\(\*\)|\s*\d+', '', city) for city in cities]
['Belin', 'München', 'Los Angeles']
</code></pre>
<p>Using <a href="https://docs.python.org/3/library/stdtypes.html#str.rstrip" rel="nofollow"><code>str.rstrip</code></a>, you need to specify all characters to remove:</p>
<pre><code>&gt;&gt;&gt; [city.rstrip(' 0123456789(*)') for city in cities]
['Belin', 'München', 'Los Angeles']
</code></pre>
