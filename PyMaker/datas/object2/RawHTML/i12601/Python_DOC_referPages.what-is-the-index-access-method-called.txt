<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>In Python, what is the <code>[1:3]</code> syntax on the second line called?</p>
<pre><code>test = (1,2,3,4,5)
print(test[1:3])
</code></pre>
<p>The second parameter ("3") seems to be the index - 1 
(3 - 1 = 2)?  </p>
</div>
<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>It's called <a href="https://docs.python.org/3/reference/expressions.html#slicings" rel="nofollow">slicing</a>.</p>
<p>The first (lower bound) and the second parameter (upper bound) are indexes. The lower bound is included, but the upper bound is excluded in the result.</p>
</div>
<span class="comment-copy">For a beginner it is a good trick to count the commas: <code>[1:3]</code> -&gt; everything between the 1st and 3rd comma.</span>
