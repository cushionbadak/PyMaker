<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>You can use a combination of <a href="https://docs.python.org/3/library/functions.html#all" rel="nofollow noreferrer"><code>all</code></a> and <a href="https://docs.python.org/3/library/functions.html#any" rel="nofollow noreferrer"><code>any</code></a>.</p>
<pre><code>&gt;&gt;&gt; rows = [[1, 2, 3], [4, 5]]
</code></pre>
<h1>[1, 2]</h1>
<pre><code>&gt;&gt;&gt; to_search = [1, 2]
</code></pre>
<h2>Check that 1 and 2 are in the same sub-list of <code>Y</code></h2>
<pre><code>&gt;&gt;&gt; any(all(x in row for x in to_search) for row in rows)
True
</code></pre>
<p>There should be one sublist of <code>rows</code> which include every <code>x</code> from <code>to_search</code>.</p>
<h2>Check that 1 and 2 are in <code>Y</code></h2>
<pre><code>&gt;&gt;&gt; all(any(x in row for row in rows) for x in to_search)
True
</code></pre>
<p>Every <code>x</code> from the <code>to_search</code> list should be in one of the sublists of <code>rows</code>.</p>
<h1>[1, 4]</h1>
<p>With <code>[1, 4]</code>, the difference between the two becomes more apparent. <code>1</code> and <code>4</code> are both somewhere in <code>Y</code>, but not in the same sub-list:</p>
<h2>Check that 1 and 4 are in the same sub-list of <code>Y</code></h2>
<pre><code>&gt;&gt;&gt; to_search = [1, 4]
&gt;&gt;&gt; any(all(x in row for x in to_search) for row in rows)
False
</code></pre>
<h2>Check that 1 and 4 are in <code>Y</code></h2>
<pre><code>&gt;&gt;&gt; all(any(x in row for row in rows) for x in to_search)
True
</code></pre>
