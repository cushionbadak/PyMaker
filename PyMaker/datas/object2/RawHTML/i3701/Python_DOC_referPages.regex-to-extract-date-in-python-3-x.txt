<p>Assuming month is always three letters (Jan, Feb...Dec), and day is always two digist (01,02...31)</p>
<pre><code>data = """*Date:* May 31, 2018 at 1:49:05 PM EDT"""
regexp = re.search('\*Date:\* (\w{3}) (\d{2})', data)
month, day = regexp.groups()
</code></pre>
<p>Or make it a one liner...</p>
<pre><code>month, day = re.search('\*Date:\* (\w{3}) (\d{2})', data).groups()
</code></pre>

<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>To answer your original question about using regular expressions: the issue is that you're trying to match groups, but <code>findall</code> returns a list of overall matches.  It's probably better to use <code>re.search</code>, and access the groups, as one commenter said.  Also don't forget to escape the <code>*</code> in your regular expression.  Here's a working example using <code>re.search</code>:</p>
<pre><code>import re
data = """*Date:* May 31, 2018 at 1:49:05 PM EDT"""
result = re.search(r'^\*Date:\* (\w+) (\d+)', data)
month = result.group(1)
date = result.group(2)
print(month, date)
</code></pre>
<p>This outputs <code>May 31</code></p>
