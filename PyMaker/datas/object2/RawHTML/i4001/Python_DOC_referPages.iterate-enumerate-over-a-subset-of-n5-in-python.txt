<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>I have a subset of ℕ<sup>5</sup>, five dimensional vectors whose elements lie in the natural numbers. This subset is defined as the product of intervals [<em>a</em><sub>1</sub>, <em>a</em><sub>2</sub>]×[<em>b</em><sub>1</sub>, <em>b</em><sub>2</sub>]×...×[<em>f</em><sub>1</sub>, <em>f</em><sub>2</sub>], and I would like to enumerate the subsets of size 1, that is the vectors (<em>x</em><sub>1</sub>, <em>x</em><sub>2</sub>, <em>x</em><sub>3</sub>, <em>x</em><sub>4</sub>, <em>x</em><sub>5</sub>) where <em>x</em><sub>1</sub> is in [<em>a</em><sub>1</sub>, <em>a</em><sub>2</sub>], <em>x</em><sub>2</sub> is in [<em>b</em><sub>1</sub>, <em>b</em><sub>2</sub>] etc. What is the best way to do this?</p>
</div>
<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>This is the <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cartesian_product" rel="nofollow noreferrer"><em>cartesian product</em></a> and you can use <a href="https://docs.python.org/3/library/itertools.html#itertools.product" rel="nofollow noreferrer"><code>itertools.product</code></a> to compute it:</p>
<pre><code>a = [0, 1]
b = [2, 3]
c = [4, 5]
d = [6, 7]
e = [8, 9]

for subset in product(a, b, c, d, e):
    print(subset)
</code></pre>
