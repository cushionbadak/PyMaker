<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p><a href="https://docs.python.org/2/library/random.html#random.random" rel="nofollow noreferrer">random</a> method does not take arguments.</p>
<p>If you would like to generate range of numbers between 0 to 1. Then you could do,</p>
<pre><code>[random.random() for i in range(10)] # To generate 10 random numbers, replace 10 with params.matrix_size.
</code></pre>
<p>Or with numpy.</p>
<pre><code>import numpy as np
np.random.random(10)
</code></pre>

<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>Error is kind of explicit, but you can also check <a href="https://docs.python.org/3.5/library/random.html#random.random" rel="nofollow noreferrer">the random.random() doc</a>.</p>
<p>As you can see, <code>random.random()</code> does not expect any argument, but you given one (<code>param.matrix_size</code>).</p>
<p>If you wants a random integer between two integers, uses <a href="https://docs.python.org/3/library/random.html#random.randint" rel="nofollow noreferrer">random.randint()</a> like <code>random.randint(0, params.matrix_size)</code></p>
