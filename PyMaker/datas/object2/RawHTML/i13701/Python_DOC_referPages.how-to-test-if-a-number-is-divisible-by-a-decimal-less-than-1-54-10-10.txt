<p>I'm trying to test if a <code>float</code> i.e(<code>54.10</code>) is divisible by <code>0.10</code>.  <code>54.10 % .10</code> returns <code>.10</code> and not <code>0</code>, why is that and how can I get it to do what I want it to do?</p>
</div>
<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>You can use the <a href="https://docs.python.org/3/library/decimal.html" rel="nofollow"><code>decimal</code></a> module to avoid the floating point precision problem:</p>
<pre><code>&gt;&gt;&gt; import decimal
&gt;&gt;&gt; decimal.Decimal('54.10') % decimal.Decimal('0.10')
Decimal('0.00')
</code></pre>
</div>
<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>The tried and true method here is to multiply your divisor and dividend by a power of 10. Effectively,  54.10 becomes 541 and 0.10 becomes 1. Then you can use standard modulo or ceiling and floor to achieve what you need.</p>
