<p>I have a dictionary like so:</p>
<pre><code>{'AAAGGG': ['AGAGAGAA', 'AGAGAGAG'], 'AAAGGC': ['AGAGAGAG']})
</code></pre>
<p>I want to step through each key and then step through the chars of its corresponding values. So something like this:</p>
<pre><code>for key in myDict:
    for eachValue in key:
        for char in eachValue:
            do something
</code></pre>
<p>Hopefully there is an easy way of doing this.</p>
</div>
<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p><a href="https://docs.python.org/3/tutorial/datastructures.html#looping-techniques" rel="noreferrer">Python Tutorial: Looping Techniques</a></p>
<p>When you have the key, use the key to look up the value.  Don't iterate through the key.</p>
<pre><code>for key in myDict:
    for eachValue in myDict[key]:
        for char in eachValue:
            do something
</code></pre>
<p>More efficiently, iterate through the items and avoid the extra lookup:</p>
<pre><code>for key, value in myDict.items():
    for eachValue in value:
        for char in eachValue:
            do something
</code></pre>
</div>
<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>This should do it:</p>
<pre><code>my_dict = {'AAAGGG': ['AGAGAGAA', 'AGAGAGAG'], 'AAAGGC': ['AGAGAGAG']})

for parent_value in my_dict.values():
    for sub_value in parent_value:
        for char in sub_value:
            do_something(char)
</code></pre>
