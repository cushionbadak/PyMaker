<p>I have a problem with strings that contain linebreaks. In order to clean them up, I use pandas replace function. However, I am unable to get it to work. I use the following code:</p>
<pre><code>test_df = pd.DataFrame(data=['Why does it not go away? \n #whygodwhy'])
test_df.replace(to_replace=r'\\n', value="", regex=True, inplace=True)
print(test_df)
</code></pre>
<p>Running the code, I get the following output:</p>
<pre><code>                                 0
0  Why does it not go away? \n #whygodwhy
</code></pre>
<p>Hence, the \n does not get replaced and I am unable to find my error. Any help is highly appreciated. Thank you!</p>
</div>
<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p><code>r'\\n'</code> is wrong, if you need <code>r</code> flag, you should use <code>r'\n'</code> or <code>'\\n'</code>.</p>
<p>That is:</p>
<pre><code>test_df.replace(to_replace='\\n', value="", regex=True, inplace=True)
</code></pre>
<p>or</p>
<pre><code>test_df.replace(to_replace=r'\n', value="", regex=True, inplace=True)
</code></pre>
</div>
<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>You should not escape the '\n' character with another '\'. Just do:</p>
<pre><code>test_df.replace(to_replace=r'\n', value="", regex=True, inplace=True)
</code></pre>
</div>
