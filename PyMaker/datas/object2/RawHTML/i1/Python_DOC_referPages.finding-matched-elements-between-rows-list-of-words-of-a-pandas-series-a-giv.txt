<p>I have a pandas series where each row is a list of words. 
<a href="https://i.stack.imgur.com/tWXRu.png" rel="nofollow noreferrer">Example Series</a></p>
<p>I have a list of words, say <em>my_list</em> = ['ball', 'apple']. I want replace all the rows of the series with words that appear both in that row and in <em>my_list</em>. What is the fastest way to do this? Can vectorization be used for this type of task? </p>
<p>I want the result to be the following:<a href="https://i.stack.imgur.com/gWNw3.png" rel="nofollow noreferrer">Desired Output</a></p>

<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>Try this:</p>
<pre><code>df = pd.DataFrame({'col1':[['apple','ball','cat'],['cat','donkey'],['elephant','apple','rhino']]}, index=[0,1,2])

my_list = ['ball', 'apple']

pd.Series([[r for r in i if r in my_list] for i in df['col1']])
</code></pre>
<p>Output:</p>
<pre><code>0    [apple, ball]
1               []
2          [apple]
dtype: object
</code></pre>
