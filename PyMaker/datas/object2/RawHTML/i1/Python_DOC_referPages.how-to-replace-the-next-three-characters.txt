<p>If the pattern has characters 'ai' or 'hi', replace the next three characters with * \ *</p>
<pre><code>string = """
Or help one fainting robin
Unto his nest again,
I shall not live in vain
"""
</code></pre>
<p>The output should be:</p>
<blockquote>
<p>Or help one fai*\*ng robin<br/>
  Unto hi*\*est again,<br/>
  I shall not live in vain.  </p>
</blockquote>
</div>

<p>Use <a href="https://docs.python.org/3/library/re.html#re.sub" rel="noreferrer"><code>re.sub</code></a>:</p>
<pre><code>re.sub(r"([ah]i).{3}", r"\1*/*", string)
</code></pre>

<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>Try</p>
<pre><code>import re

strings_to_find = ['ai', 'hi']

data = 'Or help one fainting robin Unto his nest again'
for string_to_find in strings_to_find:
    offsets = [m.start() for m in re.finditer(string_to_find, data)]
    for offset in offsets:
        data = data[:offset + len(string_to_find)] + '*\*' + data[offset + len(string_to_find) + 3:]
print(data)
</code></pre>
</div>

<p>Let's do some code:</p>
<pre class="lang-py prettyprint-override"><code>string = "Or help one fainting robin Unto his nest again, I shall not live in vain"
string_new = string.split()

for phrase in string_new:

        if (phrase[i] == "a" and phrase[i+1] == "i") or (phrase[i] == "h" and phrase[i+1] == "i"):
