<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>Django's docs say the following about their role in form creation: </p>
<ul>
<li>"preparing and restructuring data to make it ready for rendering"</li>
<li>"creating HTML forms for the data"</li>
<li>"receiving and processing submitted forms and data from the client"</li>
</ul>
<p>source: <a href="https://docs.djangoproject.com/en/2.1/topics/forms/" rel="nofollow noreferrer">https://docs.djangoproject.com/en/2.1/topics/forms/</a></p>
<p>If you want to let them do the heavy lifting and manage those three things listed above then why not? No need to re-invent the wheel unless you are doing something that requires it. It really depends on what you're building. </p>
</div>
<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>As a heavy user of django and its forms. I recommend django forms with <a href="https://django-crispy-forms.readthedocs.io/en/latest/" rel="nofollow noreferrer">django-crispy-forms.</a> This is the best package you can use to customize a form by functionality and/or layout. They have ready template forms for bootstrap too which I use for most of my projects.</p>
