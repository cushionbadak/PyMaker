<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>Here’s a cool image that sums it up pretty well:</p>
<p><a href="https://i.stack.imgur.com/5ZvHx.jpg" rel="nofollow noreferrer"><img alt="Layers 2 &amp; 3" src="https://i.stack.imgur.com/5ZvHx.jpg"/></a></p>
<p>When you use <code>srp</code>, <code>srp1</code> or <code>sendp</code>, you are expected to provide a packet that starts at layer 2, such as:</p>
<pre><code>srp1(Ether()/IP(dst="www.google.com")/ICMP())
</code></pre>
<p>Without the <code>p</code>, it’s layer 3:</p>
<pre><code>sr1(IP(dst="www.google.com")/ICMP())
</code></pre>
<p>(Behind the scenes, Scapy takes care of it)</p>
<p>Layer 2 allows you to spoof the data link (Ethernet, 802.11...) frames, but it requires you to know which one to use.</p>
