<p>This will accomplish what it is I think you need (assuming your text file is stored in the same directory and you replace 'test.txt' with your filename):</p>
<pre><code>with open('test.txt', 'r') as values:
    contents = values.read().strip().split()
    new_dict = {}
    i = 4
    while i &lt;= len(contents)-4:
        new_dict.update({contents[i]: contents[i+1:i+4]})
        i += 4

print(new_dict)
</code></pre>
<p>or this, if you want the values as integers:</p>
<pre><code>with open('test.txt', 'r') as values:
    contents = values.read().strip().split()
    new_dict = {}
    i = 4
    while i &lt;= len(contents)-4:
        new_dict.update({contents[i]: [int(contents[i+1]),int(contents[i+2]),int(contents[i+3])]})
        i += 4

print(new_dict)
</code></pre>
