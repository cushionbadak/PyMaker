<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>Dictionaries can use tuples as keys, that's exactly what is happening there:</p>
<pre><code>&gt;&gt;&gt; d = {(1, 2): 100, ('a', 'b', 'c'): 1000}
&gt;&gt;&gt; d[1, 2]
100
&gt;&gt;&gt; d[(1, 2)]
100
&gt;&gt;&gt; d['a', 'b', 'c']
1000
&gt;&gt;&gt; d[('a', 'b', 'c')]
1000
</code></pre>
<p>Comma separated values are converted to a <a href="https://docs.python.org/3/tutorial/datastructures.html#tuples-and-sequences" rel="nofollow">tuple</a>:</p>
<pre><code>&gt;&gt;&gt; 1, 2
(1, 2)
&gt;&gt;&gt; 'a', 'b', 'c'
('a', 'b', 'c')
</code></pre>
<p>Gotcha!, tuples with single items are represented with a trailing comma:</p>
<pre><code>&gt;&gt;&gt; t = 'a',  #or ('a',)
&gt;&gt;&gt; type(t)
&lt;type 'tuple'&gt;
&gt;&gt;&gt; t = ('a')  #Not a tuple   
&gt;&gt;&gt; type(t)
&lt;type 'str'&gt;
</code></pre>
