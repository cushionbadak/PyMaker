<p>I've been reading the <code>django</code> docs but this is still unclear. I have this piece of url code:</p>
<pre><code>urlpatterns = [
    url(r'^$', views.index, name='index'),
]
</code></pre>
<p>From the <a href="https://docs.djangoproject.com/en/1.10/intro/tutorial01/" rel="nofollow noreferrer">docs</a>, I know <code>r</code> means raw string, but what is this:</p>
<pre><code>'^$'
</code></pre>
<p>What does the <code>^</code> mean, what does the <code>$</code> mean? There is no clear explanation on the docs?</p>
</div>
<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>These are regular expressions.</p>
<p><code>^</code> indicates the start of a regular expression.</p>
<p><code>$</code> indicates the end of a regular expression.</p>
<p>You can look at <a href="https://docs.djangoproject.com/en/1.10/topics/http/urls/#example" rel="nofollow noreferrer">some examples</a> from the docs.</p>
