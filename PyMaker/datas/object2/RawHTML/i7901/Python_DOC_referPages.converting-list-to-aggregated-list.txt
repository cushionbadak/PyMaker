<p>Assuming that your list is a list of lists with each sublist containing a date string and of of <code>x</code>, <code>y</code> or <code>z</code>, you can first group your list by dates.  Just create a dictionary, or <a href="https://docs.python.org/3/library/collections.html#collections.defaultdict" rel="nofollow noreferrer"><code>collections.defaultdict</code></a>, mapping dates to lists of x/z/y.</p>
<pre><code>dates = collections.defaultdict(list)
for date, xyz in data:
    dates[date].append(xyz)
</code></pre>
<p>Next, you can create another dictionary, mapping each of those x/y/z lists to a <a href="https://docs.python.org/3/library/collections.html#collections.Counter" rel="nofollow noreferrer"><code>Counter</code></a> dict:</p>
<pre><code>counts = {date: collections.Counter(xyz) for date, xyz in dates.items()}
</code></pre>
<p>Afterwards, <code>counts</code> is this:</p>
<pre><code>{'2-6-2017,': Counter({'z': 2, 'x': 1}), 
 '29-5-2017,': Counter({'x': 2, 'z': 1}), 
 '3-6-2017,': Counter({'x': 1, 'y': 1, 'z': 1}), 
 '28-5-2017,': Counter({'y': 1}), 
 '1-6-2017,': Counter({'z': 2, 'y': 1}), 
 '4-6-2017,': Counter({'y': 1}), 
 '31-5-2017,': Counter({'y': 2, 'z': 1}), 
 '30-5-2017,': Counter({'z': 2, 'x': 1})}
</code></pre>
