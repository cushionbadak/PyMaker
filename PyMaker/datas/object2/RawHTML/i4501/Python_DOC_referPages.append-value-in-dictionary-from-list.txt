<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>A <strong>simple</strong> way is to iterate through <code>m_list</code> two elements at a time updating the key of <code>m_dict</code> on the way. </p>
<p>Using <a href="https://docs.python.org/3/library/functions.html#iter" rel="nofollow noreferrer"><code>iter</code></a> and <a href="https://docs.python.org/3/library/functions.html#zip" rel="nofollow noreferrer"><code>zip</code></a>.</p>
<pre><code>m_dict = {"Murder At Koh E Fiza" : "rot,r", 
         "Subedar Joginder Singh" : "grn,4",  
         "Blackmail" : "blau,9",
         "Rambo":"gelb,20"}

m_list = ['Murder At Koh E Fiza','tt','Subedar Joginder Singh','yy','Blackmail','uu','Rambo','zz']

for x, y in zip(*[iter(m_list)]*2):
        m_dict[x] += ',' + y

print(m_dict)

# {'Murder At Koh E Fiza': 'rot,r,tt', 
#  'Subedar Joginder Singh': 'grn,4,yy', 
#  'Blackmail': 'blau,9,uu', 
#  'Rambo': 'gelb,20,zz'}               j                                          
</code></pre>
</div>
<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>You can simply try range approach with one loop only :</p>
<pre><code>m_dict= {"Murder At Koh E Fiza" : "rot,r", "Subedar Joginder Singh" : "grn,4", "Blackmail" : "blau,9", "Rambo":"gelb,20"}

m_list=['Murder At Koh E Fiza','tt','Subedar Joginder Singh','yy','Blackmail','uu','Rambo','zz']

for i in range(0,len(m_list),2):
    data=m_list[i:i+2]
    if data[0] in m_dict:
        m_dict[data[0]]=m_dict[data[0]]+','+data[1]
print(m_dict)
</code></pre>
<p>output:</p>
<pre><code>{'Blackmail': 'blau,9,uu', 'Murder At Koh E Fiza': 'rot,r,tt', 'Rambo': 'gelb,20,zz', 'Subedar Joginder Singh': 'grn,4,yy'}
</code></pre>
</div>
