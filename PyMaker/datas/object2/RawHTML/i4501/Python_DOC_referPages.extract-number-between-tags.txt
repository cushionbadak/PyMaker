<p>You're not being specific enough.</p>
<pre><code>'&lt;div class="abc"&gt;+8&lt;/div&gt;'[18]
</code></pre>
<p>gives you <code>'8'</code>. This works if <code>abc</code> is <code>xyz</code>, too.</p>
<p>Do you expect that the numbers are always prefixed with <code>+</code>? (I'm guessing <code>-</code> is an option, too). Are they always inside <code>&lt;div&gt;</code> tags? Are <code>&lt;div&gt;</code> tags used for anything other than keeping these numbers? Can the <code>&lt;div&gt;</code> that surrounds a number also contain other things than the number? Could any <code>&lt;div&gt;</code> contain user-defined text that looks like these numbers in the source code? Before you can define your problem more concretely, you will risk false positives.</p>
<pre><code>[ int(x) for x in re.findall(r'&lt;div[^&gt;]+&gt;([+-]\d+)&lt;/div&gt;', s) ]
</code></pre>
