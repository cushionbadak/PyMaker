<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>It looks like the output is a string. So, you can use string slicing:</p>
<pre><code>x = 'Jun 29, 2018 12:44:18 AM'
time = x[-11:-3]
</code></pre>
<p>It's best to use negative indexing here because the day may be single-digit or double-digit, so a solution like <code>time = x[13:21]</code> won't work every time.</p>
<p>If you're inclined, you may wish to use <code>strptime()</code> and <code>strftime()</code> to take your string, convert it into a datetime object, and then convert that into a string in HH:MM:SS format. (You may wish to consult the <a href="https://docs.python.org/3/library/datetime.html" rel="nofollow noreferrer">datetime module documentation</a> for this approach).</p>

<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>Use the <code>datetime</code> module. Use <code>.strptime()</code> to convert string to datetime object and then <code>.strftime()</code> to convert to your required string output. Your sample datetime string is represented as <code>'%b %d, %Y %H:%M:%S %p'</code></p>
<p><strong>Ex:</strong></p>
<pre><code>import datetime
s = 'Jun 29, 2018 12:44:14 AM'
print( datetime.datetime.strptime(s, '%b %d, %Y %H:%M:%S %p').strftime("%H:%M:%S") )
</code></pre>
<p><strong>Output:</strong></p>
<pre><code>12:44:14
</code></pre>
</div>
