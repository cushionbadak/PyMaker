<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>You may use the * operator to unpack the sequence and print the values.</p>
<pre><code>a = {1:2,2:3,3:4,4:8}
print(*a.values())
#2 3 4 8
</code></pre>
<p>You can also customize the separator and the end using the keyword arguments <code>sep</code> and <code>end</code> respectively in the call of the <a href="https://docs.python.org/3/library/functions.html#print" rel="nofollow noreferrer">print function</a>.<br/>
For example:</p>
<pre><code>print(*a.values(), sep=',')
</code></pre>
</div>
<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>you could <a href="https://docs.python.org/3/library/stdtypes.html?highlight=str%20join#str.join" rel="nofollow noreferrer"><code>join</code></a> the <code>dict_values</code> (which you need to cast to <code>str</code>) to a string:</p>
<pre><code>', '.join(str(x) for x in a.values())
</code></pre>
