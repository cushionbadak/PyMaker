<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>You can write</p>
<pre><code>if any(color in ('blue', 'red') for color in (color1, color2)):
</code></pre>
<p>If will benefit if you have 3 variables or more. If there are only 2 of them, your variant looks fine.</p>
</div>
<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>You can use <code>set</code>, specifically <a href="https://docs.python.org/3/library/stdtypes.html#frozenset.isdisjoint" rel="nofollow noreferrer"><code>set.isdisjoint</code></a>:</p>
<pre><code>if not {color1, color2}.isdisjoint({'blue', 'red'}):
    print(color1)
</code></pre>
<p>If two sets are not "disjoint", then they have a common element. This can only be the case if at least one of <code>color1</code> or <code>color2</code> belong to <code>{'blue', 'red'}</code>.</p>
<hr/>
<p>If you want to check they <em>both</em> belong to <code>{'blue', 'red'}</code>, use <a href="https://docs.python.org/3/library/stdtypes.html#frozenset.issubset" rel="nofollow noreferrer"><code>set.issubset</code></a> or its syntactic sugar <code>&lt;=</code>:</p>
<pre><code>if {color1, color2} &lt;= {'blue', 'red'}:
    print(color1)
</code></pre>
</div>
