<p>There is a list:</p>
<blockquote>
<p>name = [('Ferdinand',)]</p>
</blockquote>
<p>And i want to get this: </p>
<blockquote>
<p>'Ferdinand'</p>
</blockquote>
<p>What should i do?</p>
</div>
<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>You have a single element <code>tuple</code> inside a <code>list</code>. To simply set your variable <code>name</code> to a <code>string</code>: <code>Ferdinand</code> you can use</p>
<pre><code>name = name[0][0]
</code></pre>
<p>If you ever have a situation where you have more than one <code>string</code> inside the <code>tuple</code> this would be one way you can access a full <code>string</code> representation of them:</p>
<pre><code>names = [('Ferdinand','surname'),('John','surname')]
for tup in names:
    full_name = " ".join(tup)
</code></pre>
</div>
<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>List Comprehensions in Lambda function in List Comprehensions :)</p>
<pre><code>[(lambda x: [print(y) for y in x])(a) for a in name]
</code></pre>
</div>
