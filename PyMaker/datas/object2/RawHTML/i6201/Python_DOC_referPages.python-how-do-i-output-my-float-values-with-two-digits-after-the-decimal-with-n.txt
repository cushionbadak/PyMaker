<p>I'm teaching myself some Python for a job opportunity. I've just started, and I can't seem to reconcile how one uses print"".format() to output floats with only two digits after the decimal. </p>
<p>I've tried putting .2f in different places; no luck. </p>
<p>This is what I have:</p>
<pre><code>def displayEmployeeData(self):
    print "Name: {:&lt;10} ID: {:&lt;10} Team: {:&lt;10} salary: ${:&lt;10} tenure: {:&lt;10} months".format(self.name, self.ID, self.team, self.salary, self.tenure)
</code></pre>
</div>
<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>it follows the format</p>
<pre><code>print '{arg_num: format} string".format(&lt;tuple of args&gt;)
</code></pre>
<p>So in your case, it would be like:</p>
<pre><code>def displayEmployeeData(self):
    print "Name: {0} ID: {1} Team: {2} salary: ${3:.2f} tenure: {4} months".format(self.name, self.ID, self.team, self.salary, self.tenure)
</code></pre>
</div>
<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>You need to use <code>{0:.xf}</code> where 0 is the argument number and x is the number of digits you want.</p>
<pre><code>&gt;&gt;&gt; print "{0:.3f} something".format(2.234234239)
&gt;&gt;&gt; 2.234 something
</code></pre>
<p>Please go through the <a href="https://docs.python.org/3/library/string.html" rel="nofollow noreferrer">documentation</a> for more formatting details.</p>
</div>
<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>You have these two ways:</p>
<pre><code>print("{0:.3f} something".format(2.234234239))
print("{0:&lt;10.3f} something".format(2.234234239))
</code></pre>
<p>or, alternatively:</p>
<pre><code>data = 2.234234239
print(f"{data:.3f} something")
print(f"{data:&lt;10.3f} something")
</code></pre>
<h3>outputs:</h3>
<pre><code>2.234 something
2.234      something
</code></pre>
