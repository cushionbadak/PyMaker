<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>You can iterate over angles and <code>break</code> on the opposite one:</p>
<pre><code>angles_1= {'angle_1': 'abc', 'angle_2': 'acb', 'angle_3': 'cab'}
shared_vertex = 'c'

for vertices in angles_1.values():
    if vertices[1] == shared_vertex:
        print(vertices, 'is a vertically opposite angle')
        break
else:
    print('There are no vertically opposite angles')
</code></pre>
<p>I use <code>else</code> clause on loop, you can read about it <a href="https://docs.python.org/3/tutorial/controlflow.html#break-and-continue-statements-and-else-clauses-on-loops" rel="nofollow noreferrer">here</a>.</p>
<hr/>
<p>And another way is to make a temporary dict and <a href="https://docs.python.org/3/library/stdtypes.html#dict.get" rel="nofollow noreferrer"><code>.get</code></a> an angle out of it:</p>
<pre><code>angle = {a[1]: a for a in angles_1.values()}.get(shared_vertex)

if angle:
    print(angle, 'is a vertically opposite angle')
else:
    print('There are no vertically opposite angles')
</code></pre>
