<p>Explanation of the pattern:</p>
<ul>
<li>\d{1,2} matches one or two digits</li>
<li>: matches ":"</li>
<li>\d{2} matches exactly two digits</li>
<li>: matches ":"</li>
<li>\d{2} matches exactly two digits</li>
<li>\s matches a space</li>
<li>[AP] matches either an A or a P, only one</li>
<li>M, the last M</li>
</ul>

<p>Use <code>regex</code> with this expression: <code>([0-9]{1,2}:[0-9]{2}:[0-9]{2}) (AM|PM)</code>. This pattern will give you two groups: one for the numbers of the time and one for the <code>AM</code> or <code>PM</code> information. This is much better than splitting the string manually. You can test it <a href="https://regex101.com/r/TozH36/1" rel="nofollow noreferrer">here</a>, and get used to using <code>regex</code>. </p>
<p>All in all you can use it like this in <code>python</code>:</p>
<pre><code>import re

p = re.compile('([0-9]{1,2}:[0-9]{2}:[0-9]{2}) (AM|PM)')
for (numbers, status) in p.match(theString):
    #prints the numbers like 04:02:55
    print(numbers)
    #prints the AM or PM
    print(status)
</code></pre>

<pre><code>import re

inputstring = '''...'''

print(timematch.findall(inputstring))
</code></pre>
<p>The regular expression in question matches any occurrence of XX:XX:XX AM and XX:XX:XX PM, and takes into account time noted as 4:00:00 AM as well as 04:00:00 AM.</p>
