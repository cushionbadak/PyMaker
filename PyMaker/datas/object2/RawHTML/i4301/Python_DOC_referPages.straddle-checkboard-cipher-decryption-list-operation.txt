<p>What I want to do is convert a string like:</p>
<pre><code>    "77 65 84"
</code></pre>
<p>To a list like:</p>
<pre><code>    ["77", "65", "84"]
</code></pre>
<p>I tried the list() function but it does not work.</p>
</div>
<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p><a href="https://docs.python.org/3/library/stdtypes.html#str.split" rel="nofollow noreferrer">Use the <code>split()</code> method of strings.</a></p>
</div>
<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>Use <code>.split()</code></p>
<pre><code>test_str = "77 65 84"
test_list = test_str.split()

for i in test_list:
  print(i)
</code></pre>
<p>So after <code>.split()</code> <code>test_list[0]=77</code>  ,  <code>test_list[1]=65</code>  etc..</p>
</div>
<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>Use split()</p>
<pre><code>&gt;&gt;&gt; my_string = "77 65 84"
&gt;&gt;&gt; my_list = my_string.split()
&gt;&gt;&gt; my_list
['77', '65', '84']
</code></pre>
<p>You can do the same thing by specifying the separator, like so:</p>
<pre><code>&gt;&gt;&gt; comma_string = "74,65,84"
&gt;&gt;&gt; comma_list = comma_string.split(",")
&gt;&gt;&gt; comma_list
['74', '65', '84']
</code></pre>
</div>
