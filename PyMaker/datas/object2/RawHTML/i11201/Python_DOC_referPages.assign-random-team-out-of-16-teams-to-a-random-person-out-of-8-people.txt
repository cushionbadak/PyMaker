<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>This can be done with <code>random.choice([List])</code></p>
<p>Example:</p>
<pre><code>import random

persons = ['Name', 'Name', 'Name', 'Name', 'Name', 'Name', 'Name', 'Name']

teams = ['France', 'Switzerland', 'England', 'Slovakia', 'Germany', 'Ukraine', 'Spain', 'Czech Republic', 'Croatia', 'Italy', 'Republic of Ireland', 'Sweeden', 'Russia', 'Wales', 'Belgium']

combinations = {p: random.choice(teams) for p in persons}
</code></pre>
<p>The result is a dictionary.</p>
<p>If you want to avoid duplicates you have to iterate over the list.</p>
<pre><code>combinations = {}
for p in persons:
    team = random.choice(teams)
    combinations[p] = team
    teams.remove(team)
</code></pre>

<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>So the thing you're trying to do is just <em>pick random "the length of <code>names</code>" elements from <code>teams</code></em>. In this case, you should use <a href="https://docs.python.org/3/library/random.html#random.sample" rel="nofollow"><code>random.sample()</code></a>:</p>
<pre><code>&gt;&gt;&gt; import random
&gt;&gt;&gt; person = ['Name', 'Name', 'Name', 'Name', 'Name', 'Name', 'Name', 'Name']
&gt;&gt;&gt; team = ['France', 'Switzerland', 'England', 'Slovakia', 'Germany', 'Ukraine', 'Spain', 'Czech Republic', 'Croatia', 'Italy', 'Republic of Ireland', 'Sweeden', 'Russia', 'Wales', 'Belgium']

&gt;&gt;&gt; random.sample(team, len(person))
['Ukraine', 'Russia', 'England', 'Croatia', 'France', 'Spain', 'Italy', 'Wales']
</code></pre>
<p>From <a href="https://docs.python.org/3/library/random.html#random.sample" rel="nofollow">the documentation</a>:</p>
<blockquote>
<p><code>random.sample(population, k)</code></p>
<p>Return a <em><code>k</code></em> length list of unique elements chosen from the population sequence or set. Used for random sampling without replacement.</p>
<p>Returns a new list containing elements from the population while leaving the original population unchanged. The resulting list is in selection order so that all sub-slices will also be valid random samples. This allows raffle winners (the sample) to be partitioned into grand prize and second place winners (the subslices).</p>
</blockquote>
<hr/>
<p>If you want to assign two teams per person, I'd suggest you <a href="https://docs.python.org/3/library/random.html#random.shuffle" rel="nofollow"><code>random.shuffle()</code></a> the <code>team</code> list, then split the list into 2 sized chunks, and put the result into a dictionary:</p>
<pre><code>{person: two_teams for person, two_teams in 
                   zip(people, [team[i:i+2] for i in range(0, len(team), 2)])}
</code></pre>

<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>Use <a href="https://docs.python.org/3/library/random.html#random.choice" rel="nofollow"><code>random.choice()</code></a>. In your case, you could have the following:</p>
<pre><code>import random
names = ['Name1', 'Name2', 'Name3']
teams = ['France', 'Switzerland', 'England', 'Slovakia', 'Germany', 'Ukraine', 'Spain', 'Czech Republic', 'Croatia', 'Italy', 'Republic of Ireland', 'Sweeden', 'Russia', 'Wales', 'Belgium']
people = []

for name in names:
    people.append(random.choice(names), random.choice(teams))
</code></pre>
<p><code>people</code> will be a list of tuples of a name and a team.</p>
