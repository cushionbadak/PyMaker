<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>You may filter the words starting with <code>@</code> using <a href="https://docs.python.org/3/library/stdtypes.html#str.startswith" rel="nofollow noreferrer"><strong><code>string.startswith</code></strong></a> as:</p>
<pre><code>&gt;&gt;&gt; s = "@PERSONSTWITTER their message. @ANOTHERWRITER their another message."

&gt;&gt;&gt; ' '.join(word for word in s.split() if not word.startswith('@'))
'their message. their another message.'
</code></pre>
<p>Here I'm firstly splitting your sentence into words, filtering the words not starting with <code>@</code>, and then joining back again.</p>

<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>Use the <code>.split()</code> method to convert the <code>string</code> into a <code>list</code> of <code>strings</code> formed from splitting the original at (by default) <code>spaces</code>.</p>
<p>Then use the <code>.join</code> method to join all the elements from index <code>1</code> on wards in the list together, separated again by a space.</p>
<pre><code>s = "@PERSONSTWITTER their message"
' '.join(s.split()[1:])
# --&gt; 'their message'
</code></pre>
<hr/>
<p>An alternative approach would be to just <code>index</code> the first <code>space</code> and slice from then on wards:</p>
<pre><code>s = "@PERSONSTWITTER their message"
s[s.index(' ')+1:]
# --&gt; 'their message'
</code></pre>
<p><em>Note that we had to add <code>1</code> to the index because <code>strings</code> are zero-based</em></p>
</div>
