<p>The names are probably the trickiest part, but if you know that the structure of city, state, zip will always be the same (i.e. no extra commas) you could use <a href="https://docs.python.org/3/library/stdtypes.html#str.rsplit" rel="nofollow noreferrer"><code>rsplit</code></a> to split the strings. Similarly pandas has a <a href="https://pandas.pydata.org/pandas-docs/stable/generated/pandas.Series.str.rsplit.html" rel="nofollow noreferrer"><code>str.rsplit</code></a> method as well.</p>
<pre><code>df
                                            Address
0          Wal-Mart Stores, Inc., Clinton, IA 52732
1         Benton Packing, LLC, Clearfield, UT 84016
2          North Coast Iron Corp, Seattle, WA 98109
3  Messer Construction Co. Inc., Amarillo, TX 79109

df['Zip'] = df.Address.map(lambda x: x.rsplit(' ', 1)[-1])
df['Name'], df['City'], df['State']= zip(*df.Address.map(lambda x: x.rsplit(' ', 1)[0].rsplit(',', 2)))
