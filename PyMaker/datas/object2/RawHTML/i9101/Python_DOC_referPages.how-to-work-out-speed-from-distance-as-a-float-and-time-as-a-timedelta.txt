<p>If I have a  distance in km float variable, and a timedelta variable of time, how can I work out speed in KM/H? I'm not sure how to change timedelta to hours as a float. An example would be:</p>
<pre><code>distance = 0.966757
time = timedelta('0 days 00:01:04')
</code></pre>
<p>Having an output of around 54Kmh</p>

<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>convert that '1:04' to seconds</p>
<pre><code>distance = 0.966757

time = datetime.timedelta(0,64)

distance/time.total_seconds()
&gt;&gt;0.015105578125
</code></pre>
<p>so you have speed in distance/second</p>
<p>per hour, you mulitply by 3600</p>
<pre><code>3600*distance/time.total_seconds()
&gt;&gt;54.380081249999996
</code></pre>

<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>Use <code>distance / ( time.total_seconds() / 3600.0 )</code> (assuming distance is already in km).</p>
</div>
<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>First of all, you have to <strong>initialize your <code>timedelta</code></strong> with the proper <a href="https://docs.python.org/3/library/datetime.html?highlight=timedelta#datetime.timedelta" rel="nofollow noreferrer">arguments</a>:</p>
<pre><code>time = timedelta(days=0,hours=0,minutes=1,seconds=4)
</code></pre>
<p><code>timedelta</code> has a method <code>total_seconds()</code> so you fetch:</p>
<pre><code>total_seconds = time.total_seconds()
</code></pre>
<p>By dividing by 3600, you get the number of hours:</p>
<pre><code>hours = total_seconds/3600.0
</code></pre>
<p>(in <a class="post-tag" href="/questions/tagged/python-3.x" rel="tag" title="show questions tagged 'python-3.x'">python-3.x</a> it is sufficient to divide by <code>3600</code> (<code>int</code>)).</p>
<p>and next you calculate the speed by dividing the distance (km) by the time (hours):</p>
<pre><code>speed = distance/hours # unit: kmph
</code></pre>
</div>
