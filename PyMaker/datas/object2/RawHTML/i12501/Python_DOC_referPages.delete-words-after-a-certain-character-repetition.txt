<p>I have strings like:</p>
<pre><code>'John Lasseter , Pete Docter , Andrew Stanton , Joe Ranft , Joss Whedon , Andrew Stanton , Joel Cohen , Alec Sokolow '
</code></pre>
<p>or:</p>
<pre><code>'Jonathan Hensleigh , Greg Taylor , Jim Strain , Greg Taylor , Jim Strain , Chris Van Allsburg , Chris Van Allsburg'
</code></pre>
<p>I want to delete everything after the third name. For example in the first string , I want to have :</p>
<pre><code>John Lasseter , Pete Docter , Andrew Stanton
</code></pre>
<p>how can I do that in python?</p>

<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>No real need to use <code>re</code> for this; just use the <strong><a href="https://docs.python.org/3/library/stdtypes.html#str.split" rel="nofollow"><code>split()</code></a></strong> method on strings and index the list that is returned:</p>
<pre><code>s = 'John Lasseter , Pete Docter , Andrew Stanton , Joe Ranft , Joss Whedon , Andrew Stanton , Joel Cohen , Alec Sokolow ' 
s.split(',')[:3]
# returns: ['John Lasseter ', ' Pete Docter ', ' Andrew Stanton ']
</code></pre>
<p>Will give you the first three names in the list, as a list. </p>
<p>Using <a href="https://docs.python.org/3/library/stdtypes.html#str.join" rel="nofollow"><code>join()</code></a> with <code>",".join(s.split(',')[:3])</code> will additionally join them together in new string containing the comma-seperated names:</p>
<pre><code>&gt;&gt;&gt; ",".join(s.split(',')[:3])
# returns: 'John Lasseter , Pete Docter , Andrew Stanton '
</code></pre>
</div>
<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>Strictly speaking you would probably be better suited by a function</p>
<pre><code>def trim_after_repeat(source, delimiter, count = 3):
    # This breaks the string into a list of strings based on your delimiter 
    pieces = source.split(delimiter)
    # This sets items_wanted to match the count you want
    items_wanted = pieces[0:count]
    # This puts the items_wanted back together
    return delimiter.join(items_wanted)

string_to_trim = 'John Lasseter , Pete Docter , Andrew Stanton , Joe Ranft ,'
print(trim_after_repeat(string_to_trim), ' , ')

# 'John Lasseter ,  Pete Docter ,  Andrew Stanton'
</code></pre>
