<p>How to obtain the index of the current item of a Python <a href="https://docs.python.org/3.7/glossary.html#term-iterator" rel="nofollow noreferrer">iterator</a> in a loop? </p>
<p>For example when using regular expression <code>finditer</code> function which returns an iterator, how you can access the index of the iterator in a loop.</p>
<pre><code>for item in re.finditer(pattern, text):
    # How to obtain the index of the "item"
</code></pre>
</div>
<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>Iterators were not designed to be indexed (remember that they produce their items lazily).  </p>
<p>Instead, you can use <a href="https://docs.python.org/3/library/functions.html#enumerate"><code>enumerate</code></a> to number the items as they are produced:</p>
<pre><code>for index, match in enumerate(it):
</code></pre>
<p>Below is a demonstration:</p>
<pre><code>&gt;&gt;&gt; it = (x for x in range(10, 20))
&gt;&gt;&gt; for index, item in enumerate(it):
...     print(index, item)
...
0 10
1 11
2 12
3 13
4 14
5 15
6 16
7 17
8 18
9 19
&gt;&gt;&gt;
</code></pre>
<p>Note that you can also specify a number to start the counting at:</p>
<pre><code>&gt;&gt;&gt; it = (x for x in range(10, 20))
&gt;&gt;&gt; for index, item in enumerate(it, 1):  # Start counting at 1 instead of 0
...     print(index, item)
...
1 10
2 11
3 12
4 13
5 14
6 15
7 16
8 17
9 18
10 19
&gt;&gt;&gt;
</code></pre>
