<p>I am trying to encode datetime format for strings that are of the form: '06JAN2018' or '31DEC2017'.</p>
<p>I think this is format = '%d[xxxx]%Y' but I don't know how to encode the month portion of it.</p>

<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>The list of these codes is in the <code>datetime</code> module's <a href="https://docs.python.org/3/library/datetime.html#strftime-and-strptime-behavior" rel="nofollow noreferrer">strftime() and strptime() Behavior</a> documentation.</p>
<blockquote>
<p><code>%b</code>: Month as localeâ€™s abbreviated name.</p>
</blockquote>
<p>In your case, <code>06JAN2018</code> is <code>%d%b%Y</code>.</p>
<hr/>
<p>If what you're actually looking to do is to encode a datetime object or Pandas/NumPy datetime array to strings, you'll probably need to do the uppercasing yourself:</p>
<pre><code>&gt;&gt;&gt; dt = datetime.datetime(2017, 12, 31)
&gt;&gt;&gt; dt.strftime('%d%b%Y').upper()  # or .str.upper() in Pandas
'31DEC2017'
</code></pre>
