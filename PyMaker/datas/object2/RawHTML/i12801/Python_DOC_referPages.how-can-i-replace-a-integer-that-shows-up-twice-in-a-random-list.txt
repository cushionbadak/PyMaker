<p>I have a list that looks like this:</p>
<pre><code>l = [random.randint(0,9),random.randint(0,9),random.randint(0,9),random.randint(0,9)]
</code></pre>
<p>but if it outputs something like this<code>[9,0,5,5]</code> what can I do replace the repeating integer?</p>
</div>
<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>If the goal is to get four unique items in random order, let Python do the work for you:</p>
<pre><code>l = random.sample(range(10), 4)
</code></pre>
<p><a href="https://docs.python.org/3/library/random.html#random.sample" rel="nofollow"><code>random.sample</code></a> is intended specifically for "random sampling without replacement", which appears to be the goal of your code.</p>
