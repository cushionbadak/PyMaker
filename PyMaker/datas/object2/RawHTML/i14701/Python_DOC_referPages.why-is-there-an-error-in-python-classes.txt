<p>Watch the <a href="https://wiki.python.org/moin/DunderAlias" rel="nofollow">"dunder"</a>!</p>
<p>Replace:</p>
<pre><code>def _init_(self, correct,gas,quant,code,age,leng):
</code></pre>
<p>with:</p>
<pre><code>def __init__(self, correct, gas, quant, code, age, leng):
</code></pre>
</div>
<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>You're calling this:</p>
<pre><code>Rectangle(correct,gas,quant,age,leng)
</code></pre>
<p>but according to your Rectangle-class, it should look like this:</p>
<pre><code>Rectangle(self, correct,gas,quant,code,age,leng)
</code></pre>
<p>So basically you forgot the <code>code</code> parameter there.</p>

<span class="comment-copy">There are numerous errors in this code.  In addition to what @alecxe pointed out, your <code>Counter.add</code> method uses two fields that do not exist:  <code>self.qusm</code>, which should be <code>self.qsum</code>, and <code>self.quant</code>, which does not exist at all.</span>
<span class="comment-copy">Also, you don't need getter and setter methods in Python.  You can safely replace all those <code>getAge</code> and <code>getGas</code> methods with direct attribute access like <code>some_rectangle_instance.age</code>.  No, you don't have to worry about "what if it changes in the future?"...  Python's <a href="https://docs.python.org/3/library/functions.html#property" rel="nofollow noreferrer"><code>property</code></a> built-in has you covered.</span>
