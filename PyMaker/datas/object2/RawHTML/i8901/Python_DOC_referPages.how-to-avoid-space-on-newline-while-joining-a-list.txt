<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>There's a simple enough solution: replace <code>\n[space]</code> with <code>\n</code>. That way all spaces are left alone and only string replaced is <code>\n[space]</code> with newline without space</p>
<pre><code>&gt;&gt;&gt; newContents = ['The', 'crazy', 'panda', 'walked', 'to', 'the', 'Maulik', 'and', 'then', 'picked.', 'A', 'nearby', 'Ankur', 'was\n', 'unaffected', 'by', 'these', 'events.\n']
&gt;&gt;&gt; print(' '.join(newContents).replace('\n ', '\n'))
The crazy panda walked to the Maulik and then picked. A nearby Ankur was
unaffected by these events.
</code></pre>
</div>

<pre><code>print(' '.join(newContents).replace('\n ', '\n'))
</code></pre>
<p>It outputs :</p>
<pre><code>The crazy panda walked to the Maulik and then picked. A nearby Ankur was

</code></pre>

<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>Use <a href="https://docs.python.org/3/library/re.html#re.sub" rel="nofollow noreferrer"><strong><em>re.sub</em></strong></a> function to remove spaces right after newline:</p>
<pre><code>import re

newContents = ['The', 'crazy', 'panda', 'walked', 'to', 'the', 'Maulik', 'and', 'then', 'picked.', 'A', 'nearby', 'Ankur', 'was\n', 'unaffected', 'by', 'these', 'events.\n']
print(re.sub(r'\n\s+', '\n',' '.join(newContents)))
</code></pre>
<p>The output:</p>
<pre><code>The crazy panda walked to the Maulik and then picked. A nearby Ankur was
unaffected by these events.
</code></pre>
<p>The above will also remove multiple spaces(if occur) after newline</p>
</div>
