<p>In <code>python 2</code>, when I use <code>chr(143)</code> I get <code>\x8f</code>.</p>
<p>But when I do the same thing in <code>python 3</code>, <code>chr(143).encode()</code>produces <code>\xc2\x8f</code>.</p>
<p>Is there a way for me to just get <code>\x8f</code> in <code>python 3</code>?
Why am I getting <code>\xc2</code> in the front?</p>
</div>
<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>You could try using a different encoding, as they produce different results. The default is <code>utf-8</code>.</p>
<p>I tried this (using <code>python 3</code>):</p>
<pre><code>&gt;&gt;&gt; chr(143).encode('latin')
b'\x8f'
&gt;&gt;&gt; chr(143).encode('utf-8')
b'\xc2\x8f'
&gt;&gt;&gt; chr(143).encode('utf-16')
b'\xff\xfe\x8f\x00'
</code></pre>
</div>
<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>You can use <a href="https://docs.python.org/3/library/stdtypes.html#int.to_bytes" rel="nofollow noreferrer"><code>int.to_bytes</code></a>:</p>
<pre><code>&gt;&gt;&gt; (143).to_bytes(1, 'big')
b'\x8f'
&gt;&gt;&gt;
</code></pre>
</div>
