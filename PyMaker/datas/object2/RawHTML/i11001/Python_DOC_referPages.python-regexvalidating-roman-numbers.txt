<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>Short answer: Because <code>re.search()</code> and <code>re.match()</code> are not the same.</p>
<p><code>re.search()</code> searches for a match to the given pattern in the entire input, while <code>re.match()</code> requires the pattern to match at the beginning of the input.
Independent of that, you can force the pattern to match only at the beginning/end of the input with ^/$. See <a href="https://docs.python.org/3/library/re.html#search-vs-match" rel="nofollow">docs</a> for a detailed explanation. </p>
<p>In your case <code>re.search(your_pattern_without_^)</code> would match <code>"QQQQXII"</code> because nothing enforces a match over the entire input. On the other hand <code>re.search(your_pattern_with_^)</code> or <code>re.match(your_pattern_without_^)</code> would not match <code>"QQQQXII"</code> because either the pattern or <code>re.match()</code> requires a match at the start of the input.</p>
