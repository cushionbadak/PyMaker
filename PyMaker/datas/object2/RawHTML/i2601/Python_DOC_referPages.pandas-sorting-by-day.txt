<p>You can set <code>Month</code> to be an ordered categorical of all the months of the year:</p>
<pre><code>months = ['Jan', 'Feb', 'Mar', 'Apr', 'May', 'Jun', 'Jul', 'Aug', 'Sep', 'Oct', 'Nov', 'Dec']

df['Month'] = pd.Categorical(df['Month'], categories = months, ordered=True)
</code></pre>
<p>Then, when you do your groupby, they will be ordered:</p>
<pre><code>&gt;&gt;&gt; df.groupby(["Month","Day"]).min()

<p>It might be easier just to get your month abbreviations from the <a href="https://docs.python.org/3/library/calendar.html" rel="nofollow noreferrer"><code>calendar</code></a> module, though:</p>
<pre><code>import calendar

months = [calendar.month_abbr[i] for i in range(1,13)]

&gt;&gt;&gt; months
['Jan', 'Feb', 'Mar', 'Apr', 'May', 'Jun', 'Jul', 'Aug', 'Sep', 'Oct', 'Nov', 'Dec']
</code></pre>
