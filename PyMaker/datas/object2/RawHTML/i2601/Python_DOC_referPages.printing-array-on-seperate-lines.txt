<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>If by "one-line" you just want everything on one line, then remove the <a href="https://docs.python.org/3/glossary.html#term-slice" rel="nofollow noreferrer">slice</a> operation.</p>
<pre><code>print(" ".join(series))
#Out&gt; 1/3/2015 4 18 43 46 55 25 3 12/30/2015 12 36 38 54 61 22 3
</code></pre>
<p>However, if you know that all your items have the same length and you want them on different lines:</p>
<pre><code>for i in range(0, len(series), 8):
    print(" ".join(series[i:i+8]))
#Out&gt; 1/3/2015 4 18 43 46 55 25 3
#Out&gt; 12/30/2015 12 36 38 54 61 22 3
</code></pre>
<p>The above uses the <code>step</code> parameter of the <a href="https://docs.python.org/3/library/stdtypes.html?highlight=range#range" rel="nofollow noreferrer"><code>range()</code></a> function to skip over 8 elements. And the slice only uses that section to print them on separate lines.</p>
<p>The following makes use of list comprehension to get your chunks and accomplish the same as above:</p>
<pre><code>for section in (series[i:i + 8] for i in range(0, len(series), 8)):
    print(" ".join(section))
#Out&gt; 1/3/2015 4 18 43 46 55 25 3
#Out&gt; 12/30/2015 12 36 38 54 61 22 3
</code></pre>
</div>
<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>You don't have to use a for-loop, you can just make two <code>print</code> calls:</p>
<pre><code>print(' '.join(series[0:8]))
print(' '.join(series[8:16]))
</code></pre>
