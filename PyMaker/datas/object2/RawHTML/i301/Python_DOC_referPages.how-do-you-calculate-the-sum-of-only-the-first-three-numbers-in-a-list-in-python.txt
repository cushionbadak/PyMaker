<p>The <a href="https://docs.python.org/3/library/functions.html#sum" rel="nofollow noreferrer">sum function</a> sums numbers in an iterable and is built into Python.</p>
<p>Thus for your situation you can just slice the list and use it.</p>
<pre><code> numbers = [2.5, 3, 4, -5]

    numbersSum = sum(numbers[:3])
    print(numbersSum)
</code></pre>
<p>result is <code>2.5+3+4=9.5</code> </p>
