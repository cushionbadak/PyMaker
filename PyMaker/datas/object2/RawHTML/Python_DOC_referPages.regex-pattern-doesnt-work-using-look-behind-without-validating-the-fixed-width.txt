<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>You may use</p>
<pre><code>rx = r'(?:(?:Ave|Rd|St|Blvd|Dr|Way|Pl|Ln|Ct)\.|Beach|Way|Walk)\s*(.+?)\s*\d{3}-\d{3}-\d{4}'
zagat['city'] = zagat['raw'].str.extract(rx, expand=False)
</code></pre>
<p>See the <a href="https://regex101.com/r/NoyCDT/1" rel="nofollow noreferrer">regex demo</a></p>
<p><strong>Details</strong></p>
<ul>
<li><code>(?:(?:Ave|Rd|St|Blvd|Dr|Way|Pl|Ln|Ct)\.|Beach|Way|Walk)</code> - <code>Ave</code>, <code>Rd</code>, <code>St</code>, <code>Blvd</code>, <code>Dr</code>, <code>Way</code>, <code>Pl</code>, <code>Ln</code> or <code>Ct</code> followed with <code>.</code> or <code>Beach</code>, <code>Way</code> or <code>Walk</code></li>
<li><code>\s*</code> - 0+ whitespaces</li>
<li><code>(.+?)</code> - Group 1 (this value will be returned by <code>.extract</code>): any one or more chars other than line break chars, as few as possible</li>
<li><code>\s*</code> - 0+ whitespaces</li>
<li><code>\d{3}-\d{3}-\d{4}</code> - 3 digits, <code>-</code>, 3 digits, <code>-</code> and 4 digits.</li>
</ul>
</div>
<span class="comment-copy">You do not need positive lookbehinds in Pandas <code>extract</code> as it only outputs captured substrings.</span>
<span class="comment-copy">You'll need to escape your dots, <code>\.</code> to match literal dots, otherwise <code>.</code> means any character.</span>
<span class="comment-copy">Try <code>.extract(r'(?:(?:Ave|Rd|St|Blvd|Dr|Way|Pl|Ln|Ct)\.|Beach|Way|Walk)\s*(.+?)\s*\d{3}-\d{3}-\d{4}')</code></span>
