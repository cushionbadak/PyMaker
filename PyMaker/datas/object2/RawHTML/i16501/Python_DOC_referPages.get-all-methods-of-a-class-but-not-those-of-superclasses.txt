<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>How would I get the methods of an instance of a class, but not those of its superclass?</p>
<p>Example:</p>
<pre><code>class A:
    def a(self):
        pass

class B(A):
    def b(self):
        pass

    def c(self):
        pass

d = B()
</code></pre>
<p>I want a way to get <code>b</code> and <code>c</code> from <code>d</code>, but not <code>a</code>. I tried using <code>dir(d)</code>, but that lists <code>a</code>, <code>b</code> and <code>c</code>, not just <code>b</code> and <code>c</code>.</p>
</div>
<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>Use <a href="https://docs.python.org/3/library/functions.html#vars" rel="nofollow"><code>vars()</code></a> on the class itself, through the <code>.__class__</code> attribute:</p>
<pre><code>vars(d.__class__)
</code></pre>
<p>or use <a href="https://docs.python.org/3/library/functions.html#type" rel="nofollow"><code>type()</code></a> to retrieve the class:</p>
<pre><code>vars(type(d))
</code></pre>
<p>This gives you a <em>dictionary</em> (specifically, a <code>mappingproxy</code>), you can always just get the names by calling <code>list()</code> on it:</p>
<pre><code>&gt;&gt;&gt; vars(type(d))
mappingproxy({'__module__': '__main__', 'b': &lt;function B.b at 0x1028a7378&gt;, 'c': &lt;function B.c at 0x103168598&gt;, '__doc__': None})
&gt;&gt;&gt; list(vars(type(d)))
['__module__', 'b', 'c', '__doc__']
</code></pre>
</div>
