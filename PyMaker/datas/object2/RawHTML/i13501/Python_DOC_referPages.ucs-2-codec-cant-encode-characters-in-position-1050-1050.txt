<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>Your data contains characters outside of the <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Plane_(Unicode)#Basic_Multilingual_Plane" rel="noreferrer"><em>Basic Multilingual Plane</em></a>. Emoji's for example, are outside the BMP, and the window system used by IDLE, Tk, cannot handle such characters.</p>
<p>You could use a <a href="https://docs.python.org/3/library/stdtypes.html#str.translate" rel="noreferrer">translation table</a> to map everything outside of the BMP to the <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Specials_%28Unicode_block%29#Replacement_character" rel="noreferrer">replacement character</a>:</p>
<pre><code>import sys
non_bmp_map = dict.fromkeys(range(0x10000, sys.maxunicode + 1), 0xfffd)
print(x.translate(non_bmp_map))
</code></pre>
<p>The <code>non_bmp_map</code> maps all codepoints outside the BMP (any codepoint higher than 0xFFFF, all the way up to the <a href="https://docs.python.org/3/library/sys.html#sys.maxunicode" rel="noreferrer">highest Unicode codepoint your Python version can handle</a>) to <a href="https://codepoints.net/U+FFFD" rel="noreferrer">U+FFFD REPLACEMENT CHARACTER</a>:</p>
<pre><code>&gt;&gt;&gt; print('This works outside IDLE! \U0001F44D')
This works outside IDLE! üëç
&gt;&gt;&gt; print('This works in IDLE too! \U0001F44D'.translate(non_bmp_map))
This works in IDLE too! ÔøΩ
</code></pre>
