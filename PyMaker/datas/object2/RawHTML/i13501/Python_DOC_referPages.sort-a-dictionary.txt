<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>Dictionaries are unordered collections. You can create <a href="https://docs.python.org/2/library/collections.html#collections.OrderedDict" rel="nofollow"><code>collections.OrderedDict</code></a> with your sorted data to keep the order:</p>
<pre><code>&gt;&gt;&gt; import collections
&gt;&gt;&gt; sorted_event_types = collections.OrderedDict(sorted(event_types.items()))
&gt;&gt;&gt; print sorted_event_types
OrderedDict([('ah', 0), ('as', 1), ('ch', 1), ('cs', 0), ('eh', 2), ('es', 9), ('oh', 3), ('os', 5)])
</code></pre>
</div>
<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>Dictionaries do not have any sense of order, they are unordered collections of key:value pairs.</p>
<p>If you want to maintain the order, you should use <a href="https://docs.python.org/2/library/collections.html#collections.OrderedDict" rel="nofollow"><code>collections.OrderedDict</code></a> . Example -</p>
<pre><code>import collections
sorted_event_types = collections.OrderedDict()
for key,value in sorted(event_types.items()):
    sorted_event_types[key]=value
</code></pre>
