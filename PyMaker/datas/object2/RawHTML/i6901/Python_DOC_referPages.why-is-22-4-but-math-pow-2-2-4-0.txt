<p>Why are not these two statements equivalents?</p>
<pre><code>&gt;&gt; math.pow(-2,2)
4.0
&gt;&gt; -2 ** 2
-4
</code></pre>
<p>Python 3.5.3 (default, Jan 19 2017, 14:11:04)</p>
</div>
<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>The order of execution of the operators (operator precedence) matters here: with <code>-2**2</code>, the exponentiation of 2 to the power 2 is first executed, then the negative sign.</p>
<p>Use parenthesis to obtain the desired result</p>
<blockquote>
<p>(-2)**2 = 4</p>
</blockquote>
</div>
<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>because the precedence of '-' is behind the precedence of '**', use (-2)**2 to calculate -2 at first</p>
</div>
<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>You can check the precedence from <a href="https://docs.python.org/3/reference/expressions.html" rel="nofollow noreferrer">the Python3 documentation</a>.</p>
<blockquote>
<p>-2**2</p>
</blockquote>
<p>calculates as : <code>-(2**2)</code> = <code>-4</code>.</p>
