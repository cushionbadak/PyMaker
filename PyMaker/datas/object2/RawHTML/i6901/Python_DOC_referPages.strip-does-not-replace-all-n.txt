<p>I'm trying to remove all <code>"\n"</code> in this string. However the <code>string.strip()</code> method does not entirely clean the text</p>
<pre><code>body = "\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\nSome text\n\nHow toremovealln?\n\t\t\t\t\tbecause notworking\n\t\t\t\t\t"
body.strip("\n")
</code></pre>
<p>The result is </p>
<pre><code>"Some text\n\nHow toremovealln?\n\t\t\t\t\tbecause notworking\n\t\t\t\t\t"
</code></pre>
<p>How to remove them all ?</p>
</div>
<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>Use <code>string.replace</code> to replace '\n' for '' (empty string):</p>
<pre><code>body = "\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\nSome text\n\nHow toremovealln?\n\t\t\t\t\tbecause notworking\n\t\t\t\t\t"
print(body.replace('\n', ''))
</code></pre>
</div>
<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>You have '\n' and '\t' to be replaced by '' and ' ' respectively. So you can use </p>
<pre><code>     body1 = body.replace("\n",'')
     body2 = body1.replace("\t",' ')
</code></pre>
</div>
<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<p>If you want to remove only the <em>duplicate</em> line feeds, you can use a regex through <a href="https://docs.python.org/3/library/re.html#re.sub" rel="nofollow noreferrer"><code>re.sub</code></a>:</p>
<pre><code>re.sub(r'([\n])\1+', '', body))
</code></pre>
<p>Or to remove them all:</p>
<pre><code>re.sub(r'\n', '', body)
</code></pre>
</div>
<div class="post-text" itemprop="text">
<h1>Use <a href="https://www.tutorialspoint.com/python3/string_replace.htm" rel="nofollow noreferrer"><code>string.replace()</code></a> not <code>strip</code>:</h1>
<p>This method will replace the old <code>char</code> with a new <code>char</code>. In your case, you want to 'replace' the <code>new line</code> <code>'\n'</code> with nothing <code>''</code>. As seen below</p>
<p><code>body.replace('\n', '')</code></p>
<p>this will return a new <code>string</code> which you can re-assign to body:</p>
<p><code>body = body.replace('\n', '')</code></p>
<p>now <code>body</code> is:</p>
<p><code>'Some textHow toremovealln?\t\t\t\t\tbecause notworking\t\t\t\t\t'</code></p>
<p>so if you finally want to remove the <code>tabs</code> <code>'\t'</code> you can just do a further <code>string.replace()</code> on them as you said above:</p>
<p><code>body = body.replace('\t', '')</code></p>
</div>
