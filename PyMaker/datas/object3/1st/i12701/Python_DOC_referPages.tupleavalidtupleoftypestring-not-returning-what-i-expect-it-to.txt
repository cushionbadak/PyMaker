In the standard Python IDE:is (5,6,7) a valid tuple? If so, why is tuple("(5,6,7)") placing every character in the string into its own spot in the tuple? Thank you so much for your help!
tuple accepts an iterable and creates a tuple of the values in the iterable. Example:"(5,6,7)" is an iterable, where each element is a single character. That is why you get a tuple of single characters. In order to create a tuple from a string, you may use literal_eval function from the ast module:
